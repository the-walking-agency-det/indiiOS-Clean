# Sync Licensing Deep Dive

*A comprehensive guide to synchronization licensing for independent artists*

---

## What Is Sync Licensing?

A **synchronization license** (commonly called a "sync license") grants the legal right to pair music with moving visual media. The term derives from "synchronizing" audio with video—literally matching sound to picture.

Unlike other music licenses that deal with audio-only uses (mechanical, performance), sync licenses specifically cover the combination of music with visual content.

## Types of Sync Placements

### Television

**Theme Songs & Title Sequences**
- Highest visibility placement
- Often renewed season-to-season
- Can generate significant performance royalties over time
- Examples: "I'll Be There for You" (Friends), "Woke Up This Morning" (The Sopranos)

**Background Score**
- Underscore that supports emotional tone
- Usually instrumental
- Can be source music (characters hear it) or score (audience only)

**Montage Music**
- Featured during transitional sequences
- Often lyrically relevant to plot
- High emotional impact moments

**End Credits**
- Less expensive than opening
- Still valuable for exposure
- Often used for "cool factor" songs

**Source Music (Diegetic)**
- Music characters actually hear (car radio, restaurant, party)
- Can be more affordable than featured placements
- Adds realism to scenes

### Film

**Opening Credits**
- Sets tone for entire film
- Premium placement
- Often uses well-known songs

**Closing Credits**
- Multiple songs common
- Rolling credits allow longer music segments
- Good opportunity for emerging artists

**Trailer Music**
- Highly specialized field
- Often uses cover versions or soundalikes
- Intense, dramatic, "trailerized" versions of songs
- Separate license from film itself

**In-Film Placement**
- Background underscore
- Character performance
- Source music (radio, TV in scene)

### Commercials

**National Brand Campaigns**
- Highest sync fees in the industry
- $100,000-$500,000+ for major brands
- Often limited term (1-3 years)
- Categories: automotive, tech, fashion, beverages

**Regional/Local Ads**
- $1,000-$50,000 depending on market size
- Often use library music or emerging artists
- Good entry point for new licensors

**Internet/Social Media Ads**
- Growing market
- $500-$10,000 typical range
- Shorter terms common
- Platform-specific (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube pre-roll)

### Video Games

**AAA Games (Blockbuster Titles)**
- Budgets rival Hollywood films
- $10,000-$200,000+ per track
- Often need multiple versions (looping, stems)
- Examples: FIFA, Grand Theft Auto, NBA 2K

**Indie Games**
- $500-$10,000 typical
- Often more artist-friendly deals
- Can offer revenue share arrangements
- Growing market with passionate audiences

**Mobile Games**
- Wide range: $500-$25,000
- Often need short, loopable tracks
- High volume potential

**VR/AR Experiences**
- Emerging market
- Rates still establishing
- Often experimental, artist-friendly

### Other Sync Uses

**Podcasts**
- Theme music: $500-$5,000
- Background beds: $100-$1,000
- Growing market as podcasts professionalize

**YouTube & Creator Content**
- Content ID claims (automatic)
- Direct licensing: $100-$2,000
- MCN (Multi-Channel Network) deals

**Corporate & Industrial**
- Training videos
- Presentations
- Internal communications
- $500-$5,000 typical

**Livestreams & Esports**
- Twitch, YouTube Live
- Tournament broadcasts
- Emerging market with unique needs

## The Sync License Fee Breakdown

### What Drives Sync Fees?

**1. Media Type Hierarchy (Highest to Lowest)**
1. Super Bowl commercial
2. National brand campaign
3. Theatrical film (wide release)
4. Network TV (prime time)
5. Streaming film/series
6. Cable TV
7. Indie film
8. Web/digital
9. Corporate/internal

**2. Usage Type**
- **Featured vocal** — Character sings along, plot-relevant lyrics
- **Featured instrumental** — Prominent, emotional moment
- **Background** — Underscore, less prominent
- **Source** — Diegetic (characters hear it)

**3. Term (Duration)**
- In perpetuity: 2-3x standard rate
- 10 years: 1.5x standard rate
- 5 years: Standard rate
- 1-3 years: 0.7x standard rate
- 6 months: 0.5x standard rate

**4. Territory**
- Worldwide: Premium
- North America: Standard
- Single country: Reduced
- Local/regional: Lowest

**5. Exclusivity**
- Category exclusive (e.g., only car commercials): +25-50%
- Full exclusive: +50-100%
- Non-exclusive: Standard rate

## The Sync Licensing Process

### From Creation to Placement

**Step 1: Create Sync-Friendly Music**

Characteristics of licensable music:
- **Instrumentals** — Vocals limit usage; instrumentals multiply opportunities
- **Stems available** — Separate tracks for mixing flexibility
- **Multiple edits** :30, :60, full version
- **Clean versions** — No explicit content for family-friendly placements
- **Emotional range** — Songs that evoke specific moods
- **Genre diversity** — Different supervisors need different sounds

**Step 2: Register & Document**

- Register with your PRO (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC)
- Copyright your compositions
- Document ownership splits
- Create cue sheets for instrumentals

**Step 3: Get Your Music to Supervisors**

**Channels:**
- Direct outreach (research, personalized emails)
- Music libraries (production music, indie artist-focused)
- Sync agents/reps
- Publisher pitching
- Music supervisor conferences/events

**Step 4: The Pitch**

When a supervisor expresses interest:
- Provide immediate access to files
- Confirm all rights are cleared
- Offer one-stop licensing if possible
- Be ready to negotiate

**Step 5: Negotiation**

Key negotiation points:
- Fee amount
- Term (how long they can use it)
- Territory (where they can use it)
- Media (what platforms)
- Exclusivity (if any)
- Credit requirements
- Payment terms

**Step 6: Contract & Delivery**

Once terms are agreed:
- License agreement signed by all parties
- Delivery of broadcast-quality files
- Stems if requested
- Metadata and cue sheet information

**Step 7: Payment & Usage**

- Invoice and collect payment
- Track usage (Tunefind, screen credits)
- Collect performance royalties through PRO
- Build relationship for future placements

## Sync-Friendly Music Creation

### What Music Supervisors Look For

**Emotional Resonance**
- Songs that capture specific feelings
- Lyrics that can mirror character arcs
- Instrumentals that set mood without distraction

**Production Quality**
- Broadcast-ready mixing and mastering
- Clean, professional sound
- No clipping or technical issues

**Flexibility**
- Instrumental versions available
- Stems for custom mixing
- Multiple length edits
- Clean/radio edits

**Clear Rights**
- One-stop licensing preferred
- No uncleared samples
- Documented ownership

**Authenticity**
- Real instruments often preferred over programmed
- Vocal performances with genuine emotion
- Unique sonic identity

### Common Sync Genres

| Genre | Common Uses | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Indie Folk** | Coming-of-age films, emotional montages | The Head and the Heart, Iron & Wine |
| **Ambient/Electronic** | Tech commercials, sci-fi, underscore | Ólafur Arnalds, Tycho |
| **Indie Rock** | Youth-focused content, action scenes | Phoenix, Foster the People |
| **Soul/R&B** | Premium brands, romantic scenes | Leon Bridges, Sharon Jones |
| **Hip-Hop (instrumental)** | Sports, urban content, trailers | DJ Shadow, instrumental beats |
| **Classical/Orchestral** | Period pieces, drama, prestige | Max Richter, Hauschka |
| **Pop** | Mainstream commercials, teen content | Various emerging pop artists |

### Creating Alternate Versions

**Essential Versions to Have:**
1. **Full version** — Complete song (3-4 minutes)
2. **Instrumental** — No vocals, same arrangement
3. **:60 version** — For commercials
4. **:30 version** — For shorter spots
5. **Sting/Logo** — 5-10 second version
6. **Clean version** — No explicit content

**Advanced Versions:**
- TV track (instrumental + background vocals)
- Acapella (vocals only)
- Stems (individual instrument tracks)
- Alternate mixes (ambient, sparse, etc.)

## Building a Sync Career

### For Independent Artists

**Year 1: Foundation**
- Create 10-15 sync-friendly tracks
- Register with PRO
- Submit to 3-5 music libraries
- Attend one industry conference
- Build relationships with 2-3 supervisors

**Year 2: Expansion**
- Catalog of 25-30 tracks
- First placements (likely small)
- Direct relationships with supervisors
- Consider sync agent representation
- Refine based on what's working

**Year 3+: Professional**
- 50+ track catalog
- Consistent placements
- Reputation in supervisor community
- Potential for exclusive deals
- International opportunities

### Working with Sync Agents

**What They Do:**
- Pitch your music to supervisors
- Handle negotiations
- Administer licenses
- Build industry relationships

**Typical Deals:**
- **Exclusive:** They represent you exclusively for sync (20-30% commission)
- **Non-exclusive:** You can work with others (15-25% commission)
- **Retainer + Commission:** Monthly fee plus percentage (less common)

**Reputable Sync Agencies:**
- Terrorbird Media
- Bank Robber Music
- Zync Music
- Round Hill Music
- Secret Road Music Services

### Music Libraries vs. Direct Licensing

| Factor | Music Libraries | Direct Licensing |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Control** | Less control over usage | Full control over terms |
| **Fees** | Lower per-placement | Higher per-placement |
| **Volume** | Higher volume potential | Lower volume, higher value |
| **Effort** | Passive once submitted | Active pitching required |
| **Relationships** | Library handles relationships | You build relationships |
| **Exclusivity** | Often required | Usually non-exclusive |

**The Hybrid Approach:**
Many successful artists use both:
- Place some tracks exclusively with libraries for passive income
- Reserve best/newest tracks for direct licensing
- Build direct relationships while maintaining library income

## Red Flags & Pitfalls

### Bad Deals to Avoid

**Work-for-Hire**
- You lose all rights to your music
- One-time payment only
- Never sign without legal review

**Perpetuity for Small Fees**
- "In perpetuity" should command 2-3x standard rate
- Don't give away your music forever for $500

**All-Media Assignments**
- "All media now known or hereafter devised"
- Way too broad for reasonable fees
- Limit to specific media types

**No Credit Requirements**
- Always require on-screen or end-credit attribution
- Helps build your sync resume
- Drives performance royalties

**Delayed Payment Terms**
- Net 30 is standard
- Net 60+ is suspicious
- Upfront payment is ideal

### Common Mistakes

**1. Not Having Instrumentals**
- 70% of sync uses are instrumental
- Always create instrumental versions

**2. Uncleared Samples**
- No supervisor will touch uncleared samples
- Clear them or remove them

**3. Poor Metadata**
- Missing or incorrect metadata kills deals
- Include: title, artist, writers, publishers, PRO, contact

**4. Being Difficult**
- Slow responses, hard negotiations
- Supervisors remember and avoid

**5. Spamming Supervisors**
- Mass emails don't work
- Personalized, researched outreach only
