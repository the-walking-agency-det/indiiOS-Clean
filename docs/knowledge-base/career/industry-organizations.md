# Industry Organizations & Conferences

The North American music industry has a complex ecosystem of organizations that advocate for artists, set standards, administer rights, and create gathering spaces. Understanding this landscape helps you navigate your career and find your community.

---

## US Industry Organizations

### NARAS (National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences)
**What it is:** The organization behind the Grammy Awards. 12,000+ voting members across the music industry.

**Membership tiers:**
- **Voting Member:** For creative professionals with significant credits. Can vote in Grammy categories.
- **Associate Member:** For industry professionals who don't meet voting requirements.
- **Student Member:** For enrolled students.

**Benefits:**
- Grammy voting rights (voting members)
- Members-only events and networking
- Advocacy on music industry issues
- Educational programming
- The Recording Academy's advocacy work on Capitol Hill

**How to join:** Application process with credit verification. Requires evidence of creative contributions to commercially released recordings.

**Why it matters:** Beyond the awards, NARAS is a major advocacy voice for creator rights and music education.

---

### RIAA (Recording Industry Association of America)
**What it is:** Trade organization representing major record labels. Not an individual membership organization.

**What they do:**
- Certify Gold, Platinum, and Diamond records
- Advocate for copyright protection
- Research and data on industry trends
- Anti-piracy efforts

**For artists:** RIAA certification is industry recognition of commercial success. The plaques matter for credibility.

---

### ASCAP, BMI, SESAC (Performance Rights Organizations - PROs)

**ASCAP (American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers)**
- Founded: 1914
- Member-owned, non-profit
- 800,000+ members
- No joining fee for writers

**BMI (Broadcast Music, Inc.)**
- Founded: 1939
- Non-profit (but not member-owned like ASCAP)
- 1.2M+ songwriters/publishers
- No joining fee for writers

**SESAC**
- For-profit PRO
- Invitation-only (selective membership)
- Smaller roster, more personalized service
- Known for strong representation in Christian, gospel, and Americana

**What PROs do:**
- Collect performance royalties (radio, TV, venues, streaming)
- License music to businesses and broadcasters
- Advocate for songwriter rights
- Provide workshops and networking

**You must join one:** You can only be affiliated with one PRO at a time as a writer. Choose based on:
- Which represents artists in your genre
- Customer service quality
- Payment timing and transparency
- Additional services offered

---

### The MLC (Mechanical Licensing Collective)
**What it is:** Administers mechanical royalties for digital audio streaming in the US (post-2018 works).

**Why it matters:** If your music streams on Spotify, Apple Music, etc., The MLC collects and distributes your mechanical royalties.

**What to do:** Sign up as a member at themlc.com to claim your royalties.

---

### SoundExchange
**What it is:** Collects digital performance royalties for sound recordings (non-interactive streaming like SiriusXM, Pandora).

**Difference from PROs:** PROs collect for composition (songwriter). SoundExchange collects for the recording (artist/label).

**Split:** 50% to copyright owner (usually label), 45% to featured artist, 5% to non-featured artists (session musicians).

---

### AFM (American Federation of Musicians)
**What it is:** Union representing professional musicians in the US and Canada.

**Benefits:**
- Union scale pay for contracted work
- Pension and health benefits (with qualifying work)
- Contract protection
- Instrument insurance

**Consider joining if:** You do session work, play in orchestras, or work on film/TV scoring.

---

### SAG-AFTRA
**What it is:** Union for recording artists (vocalists) and media professionals.

**Benefits for artists:**
- Royalties from secondary markets (cable, streaming)
- Health and pension benefits
- Contract protection

**Consider joining if:** You're a recording artist doing significant session work or signed to a major.

## Canadian Industry Organizations

### SOCAN (Society of Composers, Authors and Music Publishers of Canada)
**What it is:** Canada's PRO, equivalent to ASCAP/BMI.

**Membership:** Open to Canadian songwriters and publishers, plus international members with Canadian performance activity.

**What they do:**
- Collect performance royalties in Canada
- International royalty collection through reciprocal agreements
- Advocacy for Canadian creators
- Awards and recognition (SOCAN Awards)

**Join at:** socan.ca

---

### Re:Sound
**What it is:** Canadian equivalent of SoundExchange. Licenses recorded music to businesses and distributes royalties to artists and labels.

**Associated rights collectives:**
- **ACTRA-RACS:** For performers
- **ArtistI:** For independent artists
- **CONNECT:** For labels

---

### CIMA (Canadian Independent Music Association)
**What it is:** Trade association representing Canadian independent music companies.

**What they do:**
- Advocacy for independent sector
- Export development (taking Canadian artists international)
- Research and market development
- Professional development programs

**For artists:** CIMA runs programs like the Canada Stand at international showcases.

---

### Factor (Foundation Assisting Canadian Talent on Recordings)
**What it is:** Non-profit providing funding to Canadian music industry.

**Programs:**
- Juried Sound Recording (album funding)
- Marketing and promotion support
- Live performance funding
- International business development

**Who qualifies:** Canadian artists and companies meeting eligibility requirements.

**Apply at:** factor.ca

---

### Music Publishers Canada
**What it is:** Trade association for Canadian music publishers.

**What they do:**
- Advocacy
- Professional development
- Export development
- Rights management education

## Major Conferences & Showcases

### SXSW (South by Southwest)
**Location:** Austin, Texas  
**When:** March  
**What it is:** The biggest music industry event in North America. Thousands of artists, hundreds of showcases, film and interactive components.

**For artists:**
- Showcasing opportunities (apply through Sonicbids)
- Networking with industry from around the world
- Conference panels and workshops
- The entire music industry descends on Austin for a week

**Strategy:** Book unofficial showcases too. The official showcases are competitive; unofficial events happen everywhere.

---

### Canadian Music Week (CMW)
**Location:** Toronto, Ontario  
**When:** June  
**What it is:** Canada's premier music industry conference and festival.

**Components:**
- Conference with international speakers
- Festival showcases across Toronto venues
- Awards show
- Export and trade events

**For artists:** Showcase applications open in fall. Great for meeting Canadian industry and international delegates.

---

### Folk Alliance International
**Location:** Rotating (New Orleans, Kansas City, Montreal)  
**When:** February  
**What it is:** The global gathering for folk music. Heavy on community, light on corporate presence.

**Format:** Private showcases in hotel rooms. Artists book rooms and perform for industry and fans walking the halls.

**Best for:** Folk, Americana, roots, singer-songwriter, world music.

---

### APAP (Association of Performing Arts Professionals)
**Location:** New York City  
**When:** January  
**What it is:** Conference for the performing arts presenting industry.

**For artists:** If you're interested in theater bookings, performing arts centers, or non-traditional venues, this is essential.

---

### NAMM Show
**Location:** Anaheim, California  
**When:** January  
**What it is:** The world's largest music products trade show.

**For artists:**
- Gear announcements and demos
- Networking with instrument companies
- Endorsement opportunities
- Less about performance, more about industry connections

---

### ASCAP Experience / BMI Events
**What they are:** Ongoing educational and networking events hosted by PROs.

**Examples:**
- ASCAP "I Create Music" EXPO
- BMI showcases at major festivals
- Workshops on songwriting, publishing, and career development

**Benefit:** Usually free for members. Great education and networking.

---

### Regional Showcases

**A2IM Indie Week (New York)**
- Independent label focus
- Business conference + showcases

**M for Montreal**
- Quebec showcase with international delegates
- Known for breaking new Canadian artists

**Halifax Pop Explosion**
- East Coast Canada showcase
- Strong community vibe

**Treefort Music Fest (Boise)**
- Emerging artist focus
- Discovery festival atmosphere

---

## Key Takeaways

- **NARAS** = Grammy organization with member benefits
- **PROs (ASCAP/BMI/SESAC/SOCAN)** = Essential for collecting performance royalties
- **SXSW** = Biggest US showcase festival
- **Canadian Music Week** = Major Canadian industry event
- Join organizations that serve your career stage and genre
- Conferences are for relationships, not just performances
- Factor (Canada) provides funding unavailable in the US market

---

*Industry organizations can seem like faceless institutions, but they're run by people who care about music. Get involved, attend events, and make yourself known.*
