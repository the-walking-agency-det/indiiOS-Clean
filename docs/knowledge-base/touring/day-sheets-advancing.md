# Day Sheets & Advancing Shows

Day sheets and advancing are the operational backbone of successful touring. Mastering these processes separates professional tours from chaotic ones.

---

## Understanding Advancing

### What is Advancing?

Advancing is the process of communicating with venue staff, promoters, and local production before the show to confirm all details, resolve issues, and ensure everyone is aligned on expectations.

**The Goal:** Eliminate surprises on show day. Every question answered beforehand means fewer problems at load-in.

### When to Advance

**Timeline:**
- **Major shows/festivals:** 2-4 weeks out
- **Regular club dates:** 1-2 weeks out
- **Last-minute additions:** As soon as possible, minimum 48 hours

**Who Advances:**
- Tour manager (primary)
- Production manager (technical details)
- Artist (only for small DIY shows)

### What to Advance

#### 1. Logistics
- Load-in time and door location
- Parking for vehicles/trailers
- Hotel recommendations or accommodations
- Local transportation needs
- Catering/hospitality timing

#### 2. Technical
- Stage dimensions and load capacity
- Power availability (amperage, 3-phase, etc.)
- Audio equipment (console, mics, monitors)
- Lighting rig and control
- Backline availability
- Loading dock access

#### 3. Personnel
- Who is the point person on show day?
- Sound engineer contact
- Lighting technician contact
- Stagehand schedule
- Security details

#### 4. Financial
- Guarantee confirmation
- Merchandise percentage
- Settlement process
- Payment method (check, cash, wire)
- Tax documentation needs

#### 5. Marketing
- Poster delivery status
- Local support act
- Guest list policy
- Press/photo credentials
- Social media coordination

## The Advancing Process

### Step 1: Initial Contact

Send an advancing email with your requirements and questions. Include:
- Date and venue confirmation
- Your role (tour manager, production manager, etc.)
- Attachments: stage plot, input list, hospitality rider, technical rider
- Specific questions (see checklist below)
- Your contact information

### Step 2: Information Exchange

The venue responds with:
- Their production capabilities
- Any conflicts or issues with your requirements
- Local contact information
- Answers to your questions
- Any questions they have for you

### Step 3: Resolution

Address any conflicts:
- Equipment substitutions
- Timing adjustments
- Special requirements
- Budget implications

### Step 4: Confirmation

Final email confirming:
- All details agreed upon
- Contact information for show day
- Any remaining action items
- Day sheet will be sent 24-48 hours before

## The Advancing Checklist

### Technical Questions

**Stage:**
- [ ] Stage dimensions (width × depth × height)
- [ ] Load capacity (if bringing heavy equipment)
- [ ] Number of power circuits and amperage
- [ ] Power location on stage
- [ ] Monitor wedge positions
- [ ] Drum riser available?

**Audio:**
- [ ] Console type and channel count
- [ ] Available microphones (list specific models if picky)
- [ ] DI boxes available (how many?)
- [ ] Monitor mix positions
- [ ] Side fill availability
- [ ] Wireless microphone frequencies cleared?

**Lighting:**
- [ ] Lighting console type
- [ ] Number of conventional fixtures
- [ ] Moving lights available?
- [ ] Follow spots?
- [ ] Hazer/fog machine allowed?
- [ ] Can we use house LD or need to bring our own?

**Video:**
- [ ] Projection capabilities
- [ ] LED wall availability
- [ ] Video switcher type
- [ ] Camera inputs

### Logistics Questions

**Load-In:**
- [ ] Load-in door location
- [ ] Loading dock height
- [ ] Freight elevator available?
- [ ] Cart/dolly availability
- [ ] Load-in time confirmed
- [ ] Parking for vehicles (length needed?)

**Timing:**
- [ ] Doors open time
- [ ] Support act set times
- [ ] Your set time and length
- [ ] Curfew time
- [ ] Load-out deadline

**Personnel:**
- [ ] Production manager contact
- [ ] FOH engineer contact
- [ ] Monitor engineer contact
- [ ] Stagehand schedule
- [ ] Security contact

### Hospitality Questions

**Green Room:**
- [ ] Private dressing room available?
- [ ] Number of rooms needed
- [ ] Bathroom access
- [ ] WiFi password
- [ ] Mirror and lighting for makeup

**Catering:**
- [ ] Meals provided? How many?
- [ ] Meal timing
- [ ] Dietary restrictions accommodation
- [ ] Water/backstage beverages
- [ ] Alcohol policy

**Parking:**
- [ ] Parking for bus/van/trailer
- [ ] Parking for personal vehicles
- [ ] Overnight parking allowed?
- [ ] Cost of parking

### Financial Questions

- [ ] Guarantee amount confirmed
- [ ] Merchandise percentage (venue cut)
- [ ] Settlement time and location
- [ ] Payment method
- [ ] Tax forms needed
- [ ] Support act payment (if applicable)

## Day Sheets

### What is a Day Sheet?

A day sheet is a single-page document distributed to all tour personnel and venue staff that outlines everything happening on a specific show day — from wake-up to load-out.

**Distribution:**
- Email to all crew 24 hours before
- Printed copies at load-in
- Posted in green room
- Shared with venue production

### Day Sheet Components

City, State/Province
Venue: [Venue Name]
Capacity: [Number]

PRODUCTION MANAGER: [Name] - [Phone]

## Day Sheet Best Practices

### Timing
- Send day sheets 24-48 hours before each show
- Update immediately if anything changes
- Send reminder the morning of if significant changes occurred

### Distribution
- Email to all crew members
- Post in crew WhatsApp/Slack/text thread
- Print and post at venue load-in
- Give copies to venue production

### Format
- Single page when possible (two pages max)
- Large, readable fonts
- Clear section headers
- Consistent formatting tour-wide
- Include your logo/branding

### Content
- Include only information needed for that day
- Don't include future dates (except next day travel)
- Update actual times as they change
- Note any special circumstances

## Common Advancing Mistakes

### Not Advancing Early Enough
- Rush creates mistakes
- Venues need time to prepare
- Equipment rentals need lead time

### Not Being Specific
- "Good mics" is not a specification
- "Early load-in" is not a time
- Vague requirements lead to wrong assumptions

### Not Following Up
- One email isn't enough
- Confirm receipt of documents
- Verify all questions are answered

### Not Sharing Information
- Day sheet not distributed to all crew
- Venue contacts not shared
- Schedule changes not communicated

### Being Inflexible
- Demanding specific equipment not available
- Unwilling to find solutions
- Burning bridges over minor issues

## Key Takeaways

1. **Advance thoroughly** — Every question answered beforehand saves time on show day
2. **Day sheets are essential** — Everyone needs to know the plan
3. **Over-communicate** — Better to repeat information than leave someone out of the loop
4. **Be professional** — Venues remember easy-to-work-with tours
5. **Document everything** — Written confirmation prevents disputes
6. **Stay flexible** — Things change; adapt and communicate changes immediately
