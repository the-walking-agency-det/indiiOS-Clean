# Tour Routing & Logistics

Effective tour routing and logistics separate professional tours from chaotic ones. Smart routing minimizes costs, reduces burnout, and maximizes revenue. This guide covers North American tour planning with a focus on US and Canadian markets.

---

## Tour Routing Fundamentals

### What is Tour Routing?

Tour routing is the strategic planning of:
- Which cities to play
- The order of those cities
- Travel distances between dates
- Days off and rest days
- Market sequencing

### The Goals of Good Routing

1. **Minimize travel costs** — Less driving/flying = lower expenses
2. **Maximize revenue** — Hit markets when they'll perform best
3. **Sustainability** — Prevent crew burnout
4. **Efficiency** — Cluster dates geographically
5. **Market development** — Build markets strategically

## Market Selection

### Understanding Market Tiers

**Tier 1 Markets (Major Cities)**
- New York, Los Angeles, Chicago
- Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal
- San Francisco, Seattle, Boston, Washington DC

**Characteristics:**
- Highest guarantees
- Most expensive costs (hotels, parking, food)
- Most competition
- Require established draw
- Media and industry presence

**When to Play:**
- You have proven draw (150+ tickets)
- Supporting larger act
- Special event (album release, etc.)
- Market development phase

**Tier 2 Markets (Strong Secondary Cities)**
- Austin, Nashville, Atlanta, Denver
- Portland, Philadelphia, Detroit, Minneapolis
- Calgary, Ottawa, Quebec City

**Characteristics:**
- Good guarantees
- Moderate costs
- Strong music scenes
- Less competition than Tier 1
- Great for building

**When to Play:**
- Developing markets
- Testing new regions
- Balancing tour budget
- Genre-specific scenes (Nashville for country, Austin for indie)

**Tier 3 Markets (Smaller Cities & College Towns)**
- College towns: Ann Arbor, Madison, Chapel Hill
- Regional hubs: Omaha, Albuquerque, Boise
- Canadian mid-size: Halifax, Victoria, Saskatoon

**Characteristics:**
- Lower guarantees
- Lower costs
- Often enthusiastic audiences
- Important routing connectors
- Less frequent touring acts

**When to Play:**
- Filling routing gaps
- Building grassroots following
- Weekday dates between weekend markets
- Budget-conscious touring

### Market Research

**Data Sources:**
- **Spotify for Artists** — Where your listeners are
- **Apple Music for Artists** — Geographic data
- **YouTube Analytics** — Viewer locations
- **Social media insights** — Follower locations
- **Bandsintown/Songkick** — Similar artist routing

**Questions to Ask:**
- Where do I have the most streams?
- Where have similar artists played successfully?
- What cities have requested me to play?
- Where do I have press or radio play?
- What's the competition like?

### Market Development Strategy

**The Build Approach:**
1. **First tour:** 50-100 capacity rooms
2. **Second tour:** 100-150 capacity rooms
3. **Third tour:** 150-250 capacity rooms
4. **Continue growing** — Don't skip steps

**Why Not Skip Steps:**
- Empty rooms damage reputation
- Promoters remember poor sales
- Better to sell out small room than play to half-empty large room
- Gradual growth is sustainable

## Routing Principles

### Geographic Clustering

**The Cluster Strategy:**
Group dates by region to minimize travel:

**Example Northeast Cluster:**
```
Night 1: Boston, MA
Night 2: New Haven, CT
Night 3: New York, NY
Night 4: Philadelphia, PA
Night 5: Baltimore, MD
Night 6: Washington, DC
```

**Benefits:**
- Short drives (2-4 hours between cities)
- Lower fuel costs
- Less vehicle wear
- Crew less exhausted
- Can use same regional promoter contacts

### Natural Corridors

**Major US/Canada Touring Corridors:**

**I-95 Corridor (East Coast):**
- Boston → NYC → Philadelphia → Baltimore → DC → Richmond → Atlanta → Florida
- Dense population
- Many markets close together
- Heavy traffic (plan accordingly)

**I-5 Corridor (West Coast):**
- Seattle → Portland → San Francisco → Los Angeles → San Diego
- Beautiful drive
- Strong music scenes
- Higher costs

**I-10 Corridor (Southern):**
- Los Angeles → Phoenix → El Paso → San Antonio → Houston → New Orleans → Mobile → Jacksonville
- Longer drives
- Lower costs
- Different markets

**I-80 Corridor (Northern):**
- San Francisco → Reno → Salt Lake City → Denver → Omaha → Chicago → Cleveland → NYC
- Cross-country route
- Mountain weather considerations

**I-35 Corridor (Central):**
- Minneapolis → Des Moines → Kansas City → Oklahoma City → Dallas → Austin → San Antonio
- North-south central route

**Canadian Corridors:**
- **Hwy 401/Trans-Canada:** Toronto → Ottawa → Montreal → Quebec City → Maritimes
- **Trans-Canada West:** Vancouver → Calgary → Regina → Winnipeg → Thunder Bay
- **Hwy 2:** Calgary → Edmonton corridor

### Day-of-Week Strategy

**Weekend vs. Weekday:**

**Weekends (Friday-Saturday):**
- Best for major markets
- Higher ticket prices possible
- More competition from other events
- Book first — they fill up

**Weekdays (Sunday-Thursday):**
- Better for college towns
- Often lower guarantees
- Less competition
- Can be strong with right audience

**Sunday:**
- Often underestimated
- Good for all-ages shows
- Earlier curfews common
- Can be strong in certain markets

**Monday-Wednesday:**
- Hardest nights
- Avoid in major markets unless established
- College towns can work
- Consider days off instead

### The Routing Formula

**Ideal Show Pattern:**
```
Show → Travel → Show → Day Off → Show → Travel → Show
```

**Maximum Sustainable Pattern:**
```
Show → Show → Show → Day Off → Show → Show → Show → Day Off
```

**Burnout Pattern (Avoid):**
```
Show → Show → Show → Show → Show → Show → Show
```

**Why Days Off Matter:**
- Physical recovery
- Mental health
- Vehicle maintenance
- Laundry and personal tasks
- Relationship maintenance (calls home)
- Creative recharge

### Drive Time Limits

**Recommended Maximums:**

**Day of Show:**
- 4-6 hours maximum
- Arrive 4+ hours before load-in
- Allows for delays

**Day Off Travel:**
- 8-10 hours maximum
- Can push to 12 if necessary
- Two drivers recommended for long hauls

**Overnight Drives:**
- Professional driver required
- Sleeper bus or van
- Not recommended for DIY tours

## Transportation Options

### Personal Vehicle

**Best For:**
- Solo artists
- Duos
- Small bands (3-4 members)
- Regional tours
- Budget tours

**Pros:**
- Lowest cost
- Maximum flexibility
- No rental agreements
- Familiar vehicle

**Cons:**
- Limited space
- Wear and tear on personal vehicle
- No sleeper space
- Insurance considerations

**Vehicle Types:**
- **Sedan/Wagon:** Solo/duo, minimal gear
- **Minivan:** Small band, moderate gear
- **SUV:** Small band, more gear
- **Cargo van:** Full band, lots of gear

### Rented Van

**Best For:**
- Bands without suitable vehicles
- Tours requiring reliability
- International artists

**Pros:**
- Reliable
- Appropriate size
- No wear on personal vehicle
- Roadside assistance included

**Cons:**
- Daily rental cost ($80-150/day)
- Mileage limits (or unlimited for more)
- Insurance requirements
- Must return to specific location (usually)

**Rental Companies:**
- Enterprise (most locations)
- Hertz
- Avis/Budget
- Local commercial vehicle rental

### Sprinter Van (Owned or Rented)

**Best For:**
- Mid-size tours
- Bands wanting to tour frequently
- Comfortable long-distance travel

**Pros:**
- Good cargo space
- Can be customized
- Better fuel economy than larger vehicles
- Professional appearance

**Cons:**
- Higher rental/purchase cost
- Limited sleeping space (unless converted)
- Still requires hotels

### Tour Bus (Sleeper Coach)

**Best For:**
- Established acts
- Long tours
- Theater-level and above

**Pros:**
- Sleep while traveling
- No hotel costs
- Professional facilities
- Crew rest area

**Cons:**
- Very expensive ($1,000-2,500/day)
- Requires professional driver
- Parking challenges
- Fuel costs

**Bus Companies:**
- Bandago
- Starcruiser
- Various regional operators

### Trailer

**Best For:**
- Bands with lots of gear
- Multiple vehicles
- Long-term touring

**Pros:**
- Separates gear from passengers
- Can use smaller tow vehicle
- Lockable storage

**Cons:**
- Towing challenges (parking, reversing)
- Additional insurance
- Slower travel
- Security when detached

### Flights + Backline

**Best For:**
- Fly-in dates
- International touring
- When driving isn't feasible

**Pros:**
- Fast long-distance travel
- No vehicle fatigue
- Can reach any market

**Cons:**
- Expensive
- Gear rental costs at destination
- Airport time
- Less flexible

## Accommodation Strategy

### Hotel Types

**Budget Chains:**
- Motel 6, Super 8, Red Roof Inn
- $60-100/night
- Basic but reliable
- Often near highways

**Mid-Range:**
- Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express, Fairfield Inn
- $100-180/night
- Better amenities
- Free breakfast (saves money)

**Extended Stay:**
- Residence Inn, Candlewood Suites
- Kitchen facilities
- Weekly rates
- Good for longer stops

**Boutique/Independent:**
- Varies widely
- Character and location
- Can be great or terrible
- Check reviews carefully

### Booking Strategy

**When to Book:**
- **3+ months out:** Major markets, weekends, festivals
- **1 month out:** Most dates
- **Day of:** Last resort, use apps (HotelTonight)

**How to Save:**
- **Booking direct:** Often matches or beats OTAs
- **Membership programs:** Points add up
- **Corporate rates:** If you have business entity
- **Negotiation:** For multiple rooms or extended stays

**Room Sharing:**
- Standard in DIY touring
- 2-3 people per room common
- Establish roommate preferences early
- Consider privacy needs

### Alternative Accommodations

**Airbnb/Vacation Rentals:**
- Can be cheaper for groups
- Kitchen saves food costs
- More space
- Check cancellation policies

**Friends/Family:**
- Free accommodation
- Personal connections
- Be good guests (bring gifts, clean up)
- Don't overstay welcome

**Artist Housing:**
- Some cities have musician housing networks
- Hostels with private rooms
- Sometimes arranged by venues

## Advanced Routing Strategies

### The Anchor Date Approach

**How It Works:**
1. Book the most important date first (festival, major market)
2. Build routing around that date
3. Fill in surrounding dates

**Benefits:**
- Secures key opportunities
- Creates tour focal point
- Easier to book other dates around anchor

### The Weekend Warrior Strategy

**How It Works:**
- Friday/Saturday shows only
- Return home between weekends
- Regional focus

**Benefits:**
- Minimal time off day job
- Lower accommodation costs
- Sustainable long-term

**Drawbacks:**
- Limited market reach
- Higher per-show travel costs
- Slower growth

### The Residency Build

**How It Works:**
- Multiple shows in same market over time
- Build audience gradually
- Monthly or quarterly returns

**Benefits:**
- Deep market penetration
- Lower travel costs
- Strong local following

### Support Slot Routing

**How It Works:**
- Join established artist's tour
- Use their routing
- Access larger venues

**Benefits:**
- Exposure to new audiences
- Professional routing
- Shared costs
- Industry credibility

## Seasonal Considerations

### Weather Routing

**Winter Touring (Dec-Feb):**
- Avoid northern routes if possible
- Mountain passes can close
- Vehicle reliability critical
- Build in weather delays

**Summer Touring (Jun-Aug):**
- Festival season
- Outdoor venues available
- Tourist market competition
- Air conditioning essential

**Shoulder Seasons (Mar-May, Sep-Nov):**
- Often ideal touring weather
- Less competition
- Lower accommodation costs
- Flexible routing

### Holiday Blackouts

**Avoid These Dates:**
- Thanksgiving week (US)
- Christmas/New Year
- Memorial Day weekend (competition)
- July 4th weekend (US)
- Labor Day weekend

**Exceptions:**
- Special holiday shows
- Certain genres (holiday music)
- Specific cultural events

## Key Takeaways

1. **Cluster geographically** — Minimize travel, maximize efficiency
2. **Follow natural corridors** — Use established routes
3. **Respect days off** — Sustainability prevents burnout
4. **Build markets gradually** — Don't skip capacity steps
5. **Weekends for majors, weekdays for colleges** — Match markets to days
6. **Budget realistically** — Transportation is often 25-35% of costs
7. **Plan for weather** — Especially winter in northern routes
8. **Use data** — Stream numbers and similar artist routing inform decisions
