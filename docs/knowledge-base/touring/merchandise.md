# Merchandise on Tour

Merchandise (merch) is often the difference between a profitable tour and a losing one. For many artists, merch sales generate more net income than performance guarantees, especially in the early and middle stages of a career.

---

## The Economics of Tour Merchandise

### Why Merch Matters

**Revenue Potential:**
- Average spend: $5-15 per attendee at club level
- Average spend: $15-30 per attendee at theater level
- Top artists: $50+ per attendee

**Profit Margins:**
- T-shirts: 50-70% margin
- Vinyl: 40-50% margin
- CDs: 50-60% margin
- Posters/prints: 60-80% margin
- Accessories: 40-60% margin

**Comparison to Guarantees:**
- A $500 guarantee with 100 people = $5/head
- Same show with $10/head in merch = $1,000 additional revenue
- Merch often doubles or triples tour income

### Merch as Marketing

Every item sold becomes:
- Walking advertisement
- Conversation starter
- Social media content
- Long-term brand impression

## Merchandise Planning

### What to Sell

**Core Items (Essential):**

| Item | Price Range | Margin | Notes |
|------|-------------|--------|-------|
| **T-shirts** | $25-40 | High | #1 seller; have multiple designs |
| **Vinyl** | $25-35 | Medium | Growing market; fans pay premium |
| **CDs** | $10-15 | High | Declining but still sells at shows |
| **Stickers** | $2-5 | Very High | Impulse buys; great for tight budgets |

**Secondary Items:**

| Item | Price Range | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Hoodies/Sweatshirts** | $45-65 | Higher price point, seasonal |
| **Hats/Beanies** | $25-35 | Good margins, year-round |
| **Tote Bags** | $15-25 | Popular, eco-friendly |
| **Posters/Prints** | $10-25 | Art-focused fans |
| **Pins/Buttons** | $5-10 | Low price impulse buys |
| **Cassettes** | $10-15 | Niche but growing retro market |

**Specialty Items:**
- Limited edition items
- Tour-specific designs
- Collaborations with visual artists
- Seasonal items (holiday sweaters, etc.)

### Inventory Planning

**Calculate Initial Order:**
```
Expected attendance per show × Number of shows × Conversion rate

Example:
100 people/show × 20 shows × 20% conversion = 400 items

Breakdown:
- T-shirts: 200 units (50%)
- Vinyl: 100 units (25%)
- CDs: 60 units (15%)
- Misc: 40 units (10%)
```

**Sizing Strategy for Apparel:**
- XS: 5%
- S: 15%
- M: 25%
- L: 25%
- XL: 20%
- 2XL: 8%
- 3XL: 2%

**Buffer Stock:**
- Order 20-30% more than calculated
- Better to have leftovers than sellouts
- Leftovers can be sold online or on next tour

### Design Considerations

**What Sells:**
- Bold, readable designs
- Artist name/logo prominent
- Tour dates on back (commemorative)
- Multiple color options
- Unisex sizing

**What Doesn't Sell:**
- Overly complex designs
- Inside jokes fans won't get
- Poor quality materials
- Designs that don't match your aesthetic

**Design Investment:**
- Professional designers: $200-1,000 per design
- Worth the investment for quality
- Fans notice and pay for good design
- Consider limited editions to create urgency

## Manufacturing

### Finding Suppliers

**Screen Printers (Apparel):**
- Local printers: Better for small runs, relationship building
- Online services (Printful, Custom Ink): Convenience, consistency
- Tour merch specialists: Industry knowledge, quality

**Vinyl/CD Manufacturing:**
- United Record Pressing (US)
- Pirates Press
- Local pressing plants
- CD duplication services

**Evaluating Suppliers:**
- Request samples before large orders
- Check turnaround times
- Understand shipping costs
- Verify re-order speed

### Production Timelines

**Apparel:**
- Design approval: 1-2 weeks
- Production: 2-4 weeks
- Shipping: 1-2 weeks
- **Total: 4-8 weeks before tour**

**Vinyl:**
- Mastering and test pressings: 2-4 weeks
- Production: 8-12 weeks
- Shipping: 1-2 weeks
- **Total: 3-4 months before tour**

**CDs:**
- Production: 1-2 weeks
- Shipping: 1 week
- **Total: 2-3 weeks**

### Quality Control

**Inspect Every Order:**
- Check print quality
- Verify sizing
- Count quantities
- Test vinyl/CD playback
- Check packaging

**Common Issues:**
- Color mismatches
- Sizing inconsistencies
- Printing errors
- Warped vinyl
- Defective CDs

## Merch Booth Setup

### Physical Setup

**Table Requirements:**
- Sturdy 6-8 foot table
- Tablecloth with branding
- Professional display (not cardboard boxes)
- Good lighting
- Clear pricing signage

**Display Options:**
- Grid walls for hanging shirts
- Record crates for vinyl
- Tiered displays for smaller items
- iPad/tablet for digital catalog
- Square/card reader for payments

**Essential Supplies:**
- Shopping bags (branded if possible)
- Receipt book or digital system
- Pens and markers
- Tape and scissors
- Extra price tags
- Inventory tracking sheets
- Cash box with change

### Payment Processing

**Cash:**
- Start with $200-300 in change
- $1s, $5s, $10s, $20s
- Secure cash box
- Regular cash drops to secure location

**Card Payments:**
- Square, Stripe, or PayPal reader
- Test before tour
- Backup battery/power bank
- Receipt options (email or text)

**Mobile Payment:**
- Venmo, Cash App for tips/add-ons
- Display QR codes
- Consider fees in pricing

### Pricing Strategy

**Standard Pricing:**
- T-shirts: $25-35 (cost: $8-12)
- Hoodies: $45-65 (cost: $18-25)
- Vinyl: $25-35 (cost: $12-18)
- CDs: $10-15 (cost: $3-5)
- Stickers: $2-5 (cost: $0.25-0.50)

**Bundle Deals:**
- Shirt + Vinyl: Save $5
- Shirt + CD: Save $3
- "Everything" bundle: Significant discount
- Increases average transaction value

**Dynamic Pricing:**
- Last night of tour: "Everything must go" discounts
- Damaged goods: Discount bin
- Limited editions: Premium pricing

## Staffing the Merch Table

### DIY Approach

**Who Sells:**
- Band member (before/after set)
- Friend/family member
- Local helper hired per show

**Pros:**
- No additional salary
- Personal connection with fans
- Flexible

**Cons:**
- Band member can't do other tasks
- Inconsistent availability
- Less professional presentation

### Dedicated Merch Person

**Role:**
- Set up and break down booth
- Sell throughout show
- Track inventory
- Handle cash/card transactions
- Report sales and inventory

**Compensation:**
- DIY level: $50-100/show + per diem
- Club level: $100-150/show + per diem
- Theater+: $150-250/show + per diem
- Or: Commission (10-15% of sales)

**Qualities to Look For:**
- Friendly and engaging
- Trustworthy with cash
- Organized
- Knowledgeable about your music
- Self-motivated

### Merch Person Best Practices

**Before Doors:**
- Arrive 2 hours before doors
- Set up display attractively
- Test payment systems
- Count starting inventory
- Get cash change

**During Show:**
- Engage every potential customer
- Upsell bundles
- Track sales in real-time
- Secure cash regularly
- Take photos for social media

**After Show:**
- Stay open until crowd clears
- Pack remaining inventory securely
- Count and reconcile sales
- Secure cash and records
- Report to tour manager

## Inventory Management

### Tracking Systems

**Simple Spreadsheet:**
```
| Item | Size | Start | Sold | Left | Price | Revenue |
|------|------|-------|------|------|-------|---------|
| Tee A | M | 20 | 12 | 8 | $30 | $360 |
| Tee A | L | 20 | 15 | 5 | $30 | $450 |
| Vinyl | - | 30 | 18 | 12 | $30 | $540 |
```

**Apps and Software:**
- Square inventory tracking
- Shopify POS
- Custom tour management apps
- Master Tour

### Daily Reconciliation

**End of Show Routine:**
1. Count all remaining inventory
2. Calculate items sold
3. Reconcile with cash/card sales
4. Note any discrepancies
5. Update master inventory sheet
6. Report to tour manager

**Weekly Inventory:**
- Full count of all items
- Identify what's selling/fast
- Plan re-orders if needed
- Adjust display/promotion

### Reordering on Tour

**When to Reorder:**
- Item sells 50%+ in first week
- Multiple sizes sold out
- High-demand items running low

**How to Reorder:**
- Ship to upcoming venue/hotel
- Coordinate with local friends/fans
- Plan around routing
- Consider shipping costs vs. lost sales

## Venue Relationships

### Merch Agreements

**Standard Venue Cut:**
- 0%: DIY spaces, some theaters
- 10%: Common for clubs
- 15-20%: Some venues, arenas
- 25%+: Uncommon, negotiate if possible

**What's Negotiable:**
- Percentage rate
- Minimum guarantee vs. percentage
- Staffing (venue provides seller?)
- Location of booth
- Payment timing

### Venue Provided Sellers

**Pros:**
- No need to bring merch person
- Local knowledge
- Venue handles staffing

**Cons:**
- Less control over presentation
- May not know your music
- Commission still applies
- Split focus with other duties

### Merch Booth Location

**Ideal Placement:**
- Near entrance/exit
- High traffic area
- Good lighting
- Near stage (for after-show rush)
- Secure (can be monitored)

**Advancing Merch:**
- Confirm booth location in advance
- Specify size requirements
- Request electrical if needed
- Confirm venue's percentage

## Legal and Financial

### Sales Tax

**Requirements:**
- Collect sales tax in each state/province
- Rates vary by location
- Some venues handle collection
- Others require you to manage

**Solutions:**
- Use Square (calculates tax automatically)
- Hire tour accountant
- Simplified: Price includes tax
- Register for permits in tour states

### Inventory Accounting

**Cost of Goods Sold (COGS):**
- Track manufacturing costs
- Shipping to tour
- Venue percentages
- Calculate true profit margins

**Tax Deductions:**
- Manufacturing costs
- Shipping
- Display materials
- Merch person salaries
- Unsold inventory (at tour end)

## Merch Best Practices

### Do's

- **Invest in quality** — Fans remember cheap shirts that shrink
- **Offer variety** — Different price points and styles
- **Create urgency** — Limited editions, tour-only items
- **Make it easy** — Clear pricing, multiple payment options
- **Engage fans** — Merch person should be friendly
- **Track everything** — Know what's selling
- **Protect inventory** — Secure storage, insurance

### Don'ts

- **Overspend on first run** — Test designs before large orders
- **Ignore sizing** — Stock inclusive sizes
- **Forget display** — Presentation affects sales
- **Neglect online** — Post-tour sales matter
- **Skip reconciliation** — Discrepancies indicate problems
- **Underestimate shipping** — Factor into pricing

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Merch is essential revenue** — Often more than guarantees
2. **Quality over quantity** — Better to have fewer great designs
3. **Plan inventory carefully** — Use data, not guesses
4. **Staff the booth professionally** — A good seller pays for themselves
5. **Track everything** — Data drives better decisions
6. **Price strategically** — Bundles increase average sale
7. **Integrate online and offline** — They support each other
