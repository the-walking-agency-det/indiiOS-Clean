# Tour Accounting

Money management on the road requires discipline, organization, and clear systems. This guide covers per diems, settlements, expense tracking, and financial best practices for touring artists.

---

## Tour Budgeting

### Creating the Pre-Tour Budget

Before booking any dates, build a comprehensive budget to understand your break-even point.

#### Fixed Costs (Same regardless of show count)

| Category | Typical Costs | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| **Vehicle** | $3,000-15,000/month | Van rental, bus lease, or depreciation |
| **Insurance** | $500-2,000/month | Vehicle, gear, liability, cancellation |
| **Crew Salaries** | $2,000-10,000/week | TM, PM, merch, drivers |
| **Equipment** | Variable | Rentals, repairs, consumables |

#### Variable Costs (Scale with tour length/show count)

| Category | Per Day/Show | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| **Gas** | $50-300/day | Depends on vehicle and routing |
| **Hotels** | $100-400/night | Varies by city tier |
| **Per Diems** | $25-75/person/day | Food and incidentals |
| **Tolls/Parking** | $20-100/day | Route dependent |
| **Meals (catered)** | $150-500/day | If provided |

#### Revenue Sources

| Source | Typical Range | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **Guarantees** | $500-50,000/show | Based on draw |
| **Merchandise** | $5-50/head | Highly variable |
| **VIP/Upgrades** | $20-200/ticket | Meet & greets, early entry |

### Break-Even Analysis

Calculate your daily burn rate:
```
Daily Fixed Costs = (Monthly Fixed ÷ 30)
Daily Variable Costs = Sum of daily expenses
Daily Burn Rate = Fixed + Variable

Break-Even per Show = Daily Burn Rate ÷ Number of Shows per Day
```

**Example:**
- Van rental: $3,000/month = $100/day
- Insurance: $1,000/month = $33/day
- Gas: $150/day
- Hotels: $200/night
- Per diems (4 people × $40): $160/day
- **Daily Burn Rate: $643**

For a 30-day tour with 20 shows:
- Average cost per show day: $643
- Plus non-show days (10): $643 × 10 = $6,430 ÷ 20 shows = $321 per show
- **Total cost per show: $964**

You need to average $964 per show just to break even (before merchandise).

## Per Diems

### What Are Per Diems?

Per diems (Latin for "per day") are daily allowances paid to touring personnel to cover meals and incidental expenses while on the road.

### Standard Per Diem Rates (2024-2025)

**US Federal Rates (for reference):**
- Low-cost areas: $59-64/day
- High-cost areas (NYC, SF, LA): $74-79/day

**Music Industry Standards:**
- **DIY/Entry level:** $20-30/day
- **Club level:** $30-40/day
- **Theater level:** $40-50/day
- **Arena level:** $50-75/day

### Per Diem Structures

**1. Cash in Hand**
- Paid daily or weekly in cash
- Recipient keeps whatever they don't spend
- Simple but requires cash management

**2. Daily Add to Payroll**
- Added to regular paycheck
- Taxed as income
- Cleaner accounting but less immediate

**3. Non-Taxable Per Diem (US)**
- Paid under accountable plan rules
- Not taxable if under federal rates
- Requires documentation
- Consult with accountant for setup

### Per Diem Best Practices

**For Artists:**
- Pay per diems consistently and on time
- Never make crew ask for their per diem
- Consider half per diem on travel days
- Full per diem on show days and days off on tour

**For Crew:**
- Budget your per diems — they must last the whole tour
- Save receipts if any tax implications
- Don't blow it all on day one

### Partial Per Diems

Common variations:
- **3/4 per diem:** Travel days with some meals provided
- **1/2 per diem:** Short travel days or days off at home
- **No per diem:** Days off at home, non-tour days

## Settlements

### What is a Settlement?

The settlement is the final accounting of a show's finances, conducted after the performance (typically immediately after or the next morning). It determines how much the artist is paid.

### The Settlement Process

#### 1. Gather Documentation

**From the venue/promoter:**
- Box office statement (tickets sold by type)
- Door count (actual attendance)
- Merchandise sales report (if venue handles)
- Expense receipts (sound, lights, security, etc.)

**From your side:**
- Contract copy
- Guest list count
- Any agreed-upon deductions

#### 2. Calculate Gross Revenue

```
Ticket Revenue:
- Advance tickets sold: [Count] × [Price] = $[Amount]
- Door tickets sold: [Count] × [Price] = $[Amount]
- VIP/Upgrades: [Count] × [Price] = $[Amount]
Total Ticket Revenue: $[Amount]
```

#### 3. Deduct Expenses

**Typical Deductions:**
- Advertising/promotion
- Sound engineer
- Lighting technician
- Security
- Stagehands
- Ticket printing
- Venue rental (if applicable)
- Taxes (if applicable)

**Verify each deduction:**
- Was it agreed to in the contract?
- Is the amount reasonable?
- Is there documentation?

#### 4. Calculate Net and Split

**For Door Deals:**
```
Gross Revenue: $10,000
Less Expenses: -$2,000
Net Revenue: $8,000

Artist Share (80%): $6,400
Venue Share (20%): $1,600
```

**For Versus Deals:**
```
Guarantee: $5,000
Net Revenue: $8,000
Artist Percentage (80%): $6,400

Artist receives: $6,400 (higher than guarantee)
```

**For Flat Guarantees:**
```
Artist receives: $5,000 (regardless of revenue)
```

#### 5. Handle Payment

**Payment Methods:**
- **Cash:** Count together, get receipt
- **Check:** Verify amount, get receipt
- **Wire transfer:** Confirm timing, get confirmation

**Always get a settlement sheet signed by both parties.**

### Settlement Best Practices

**Before the Show:**
- Have contract accessible
- Know your deal terms cold
- Confirm expense caps if applicable
- Bring a calculator and pen

**During Settlement:**
- Stay calm and professional
- Question anything unclear
- Don't sign until you understand everything
- Keep a copy of the signed settlement

**After Settlement:**
- Secure cash immediately
- Photograph checks before depositing
- Record all figures in your tour accounting
- Address any disputes same day if possible

## Expense Tracking

### Why Track Expenses?

- **Tax deductions** — Most tour expenses are deductible
- **Budget management** — Know if you're on track
- **Future planning** — Data for future tour budgets
- **Transparency** — Share with band members, management, label

### What to Track

**Transportation:**
- Gas receipts
- Tolls and parking
- Vehicle rental/lease
- Flights
- Ground transportation (taxis, rideshares, rental cars)
- Vehicle maintenance and repairs

**Accommodation:**
- Hotel receipts
- Airbnb/rental receipts
- Late checkout fees
- Parking at hotels

**Food:**
- Meals (when not covered by per diem)
- Catering receipts
- Green room hospitality
- Water and beverages for travel

**Production:**
- Equipment rentals
- Backline hire
- Consumables (strings, drumsticks, cables)
- Repairs

**Personnel:**
- Per diems paid
- Crew salaries
- Union fees

**Marketing:**
- Poster printing
- Local advertising
- Promotional materials

**Administrative:**
- Phone/internet
- Accounting/legal fees
- Insurance
- Work permits/visas

### Tracking Methods

**1. Spreadsheet Method**

Simple but effective:
```
| Date | Category | Description | Amount | Receipt | Notes |
|------|----------|-------------|--------|---------|-------|
| 3/1  | Gas      | Shell, I-95   | $45.00 | Y       | Van   |
| 3/1  | Hotel    | Motel 6       | $89.00 | Y       | 2 rms |
```

**2. Apps and Software**
- **Expensify** — Receipt scanning, reporting
- **QuickBooks** — Full accounting integration
- **Wave** — Free option for basic tracking
- **Tour accounting apps** — Master Tour, etc.

**3. Envelope Method**
- Physical envelopes for each category
- Receipts stored by type
- Totals calculated at tour end
- Good backup to digital methods

### Receipt Management

**Best Practices:**
- Get a receipt for everything
- Photograph receipts immediately (ink fades)
- Note date, location, and purpose on each receipt
- Store digital copies in cloud storage
- Keep physical backups for major expenses

**Required Information:**
- Date
- Vendor name
- Itemized list (not just "merchandise")
- Amount
- Payment method

## Tour Accounting Systems

### Daily Accounting Routine

**Morning (15 minutes):**
- Review previous day's expenses
- Enter receipts into tracking system
- Verify per diems distributed

**Post-Show (30 minutes):**
- Complete settlement
- Record payment received
- Note any discrepancies
- Update daily totals

**Weekly (1 hour):**
- Reconcile all accounts
- Review budget vs. actual
- Adjust upcoming spending if needed
- Prepare any necessary wires/transfers

### Cash Management

**Cash Box System:**
- Lockbox for tour cash
- Starting float for change
- Daily reconciliation
- Secure storage (hotel safe, locked vehicle)

**Cash Security:**
- Never leave cash in vehicles overnight
- Split large amounts among trusted personnel
- Deposit regularly (don't carry excessive cash)
- Use hotel safes when available

### Bank Accounts and Cards

**Tour Account:**
- Separate checking account for tour funds
- Debit card for expenses
- Online access for wire transfers
- Multiple signers if needed

**Credit Cards:**
- Business card for larger expenses
- Points/rewards for hotels/flights
- Easy expense tracking through statements
- Backup if cash flow is tight

## Tax Considerations

### US Tax Basics

**Deductible Expenses:**
- Transportation
- Lodging
- 50% of meals
- Equipment and repairs
- Insurance
- Professional fees (agent, manager, accountant)
- Work permits and visas

**Record Keeping:**
- Keep records for 7 years
- Separate business and personal expenses
- Document business purpose for all expenses

**1099s:**
- Issue to crew members paid $600+
- Due January 31
- Requires W-9 forms on file

### Canadian Tax Basics

**Similar deductions to US**
- GST/HST considerations for merchandise
- Different per diem rules
- T4A forms for Canadian crew

### International Touring

- Withholding taxes on foreign income
- Tax treaty benefits
- Foreign tax credits
- Professional advice essential

## Financial Red Flags

### Warning Signs

- **Venue won't provide box office statement** — Insist on documentation
- **Expenses exceed agreed caps** — Reference your contract
- **No settlement offered** — Don't leave without settling
- **Cash payment without receipt** — Get written confirmation
- **Disappearing per diems** — Track and reconcile daily

### Protecting Yourself

- Always get contracts in writing
- Never perform without a signed agreement
- Keep copies of all settlements
- Document everything
- Trust but verify

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Budget before you book** — Know your break-even point
2. **Track every dollar** — Expenses add up; deductions save money
3. **Settlements are business meetings** — Professional, documented, thorough
4. **Per diems are not optional** — Crew depend on them
5. **Cash requires discipline** — Secure it, track it, deposit it
6. **Taxes are real** — Keep records, claim deductions, stay compliant
7. **Transparency builds trust** — Share financials with your team
