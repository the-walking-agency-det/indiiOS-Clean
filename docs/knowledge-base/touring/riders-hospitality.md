# Riders & Hospitality

Riders are the documents that specify your technical, hospitality, and production requirements for each show. A well-crafted rider sets clear expectations and helps ensure consistent conditions night after night.

---

## Types of Riders

### 1. Technical Rider

Specifies all audio, lighting, video, and staging requirements.

**Key Components:**
- Stage plot (diagram of stage setup)
- Input list (microphone and channel assignments)
- Audio equipment specifications
- Lighting requirements
- Power requirements
- Backline needs
- Video/projection needs

### 2. Hospitality Rider

Details your requirements for dressing rooms, meals, and amenities.

**Key Components:**
- Dressing room requirements
- Catering specifications
- Beverage requests
- Special dietary needs
- Amenities (WiFi, towels, etc.)

### 3. Production Rider

Covers logistics, scheduling, and personnel requirements.

**Key Components:**
- Load-in and soundcheck times
- Crew requirements
- Security specifications
- Parking needs
- Local runner requirements

## The Technical Rider

### Stage Plot

A stage plot is a visual diagram showing:
- Where each musician stands
- Instrument and amplifier placement
- Microphone positions
- Monitor placement
- Power and cable routing

**Creating a Stage Plot:**

1. **Use software:**
   - Stage Plot Pro (industry standard)
   - OmniGraffle
   - Adobe Illustrator
   - Even PowerPoint or Google Slides

2. **Include these elements:**
   - Stage dimensions (if known)
   - Drum riser position
   - Each band member's position
   - All instruments and amplifiers
   - Microphone positions (numbered to match input list)
   - Monitor wedge positions
   - Power drop locations
   - Any special staging (platforms, risers, etc.)

3. **Label clearly:**
   - Band member names
   - Instrument names
   - Channel numbers
   - Directional indicators (upstage, downstage)

### Input List (Channel List)

The input list specifies every audio input needed, in the order you want them on the mixing console.

**Standard Format:**
```
CH | INPUT | MIC/STAND | NOTES
─────────────────────────────────────
1  | Kick Drum | Beta 52 | Internal
2  | Snare Top | SM57 | 
3  | Snare Bottom | SM57 | 
4  | Hi-Hat | SM81 | 
5  | Rack Tom 1 | E604 | 
6  | Rack Tom 2 | E604 | 
7  | Floor Tom | E604 | 
8  | Overhead L | KSM32 | 
9  | Overhead R | KSM32 | 
10 | Bass DI | DI | Active
11 | Bass Amp | D6 | 
12 | Guitar 1 | SM57 | On cab
13 | Guitar 2 | SM57 | On cab
14 | Keys L | DI | 
15 | Keys R | DI | 
16 | Lead Vox | Beta 58 | On stand
17 | BGV 1 | SM58 | On stand
18 | BGV 2 | SM58 | On stand
```

**Best Practices:**
- List inputs in logical order (drums, bass, guitars, keys, vocals)
- Specify preferred microphones but note acceptable alternatives
- Include stand requirements
- Note any phantom power needs
- Keep it to 24 channels or less for club shows

### Audio Requirements

**Console:**
- Minimum channel count
- Preferred brand/model
- Required features (groups, effects, recording outputs)

**Monitors:**
- Number of monitor mixes needed
- Wedges vs. in-ears
- Side fill requirements
- Drum fill requirements

**Outboard Gear:**
- Preferred compressors
- Effects units
- Direct boxes (active vs. passive)
- Wireless microphone systems

### Lighting Requirements

**Basic Club Package:**
- Minimum fixture count
- Console type
- Follow spots (if needed)
- Hazer/fog (if used)

**Advanced Productions:**
- Moving light specifications
- LED video wall requirements
- Timecoded sequences
- House LD vs. touring LD

### Power Requirements

**Calculate total amperage needed:**
- Guitar amplifiers: 2-5 amps each
- Bass amplifiers: 3-8 amps
- Keyboard rigs: 2-10 amps
- Drum machines/samplers: 1-2 amps
- Backline: Varies significantly

**Specify:**
- Total amperage required
- Voltage (110V US/Canada, 220V Europe)
- Circuit distribution needs
- 3-phase power requirements (for large productions)

## The Hospitality Rider

### Dressing Room Requirements

**Basic Needs:**
- Private, secure space
- Adequate seating for all personnel
- Full-length mirror
- Adequate lighting
- Climate control
- Clean bathroom access

**Standard Requests by Level:**

**DIY/Club Level:**
- 1 private dressing room
- Seating for 4-6 people
- Access to water
- Bathroom access

**Theater Level:**
- 2 dressing rooms (artist + crew)
- Seating for 8-10 people
- Hot water/tea station
- WiFi access
- Towels

**Arena Level:**
- Multiple dressing rooms
- Private artist suite
- Crew lounge
- Catering area
- Private bathrooms
- Wardrobe space

### Catering Specifications

**Meal Timing:**
- Specify when meals should be served
- Relative to load-in, soundcheck, doors, performance
- Late-night food for post-show

**Hot Meals:**
- Number of meals needed
- Timing
- Dietary restrictions
- Style (buffet, plated, etc.)

**Common Hot Meal Options:**
- Protein options (chicken, fish, vegetarian)
- Starches (rice, potatoes, pasta)
- Vegetables
- Salad
- Bread/rolls

**Catering Red Flags:**
- Pizza every night (not sustainable)
- No vegetarian/vegan options
- Unhealthy options only
- Late or missing meals

### Beverage Requirements

**Standard Beverage Package:**

**Water:**
- Bottled water (still and sparkling)
- Quantity per person
- Room temperature and chilled

**Non-Alcoholic:**
- Soft drinks
- Juices
- Coffee/tea setup
- Electrolyte drinks

**Alcoholic (if requested):**
- Beer (local options preferred)
- Wine (red and white)
- Spirits (specify if needed)
- **Note:** Some venues have restrictions on providing alcohol

**Specialty Requests:**
- Specific brands
- Local/regional options
- Rider drinks (specific cocktails)

### The "Diva" Items (Use Sparingly)

Famous rider requests that became legends:
- Van Halen's "no brown M&Ms" (actually a safety check)
- Specific flower arrangements
- Exact room temperatures
- Specific brands of everything

**Reality Check:**
- Request what you actually need
- Be reasonable — venues have budgets
- Prioritize health and performance needs
- Remember: word travels about difficult artists

## The Production Rider

### Scheduling Requirements

**Load-In:**
- Preferred arrival time
- Minimum setup time needed
- Loading dock access requirements

**Soundcheck:**
- Minimum duration (typically 60-90 minutes)
- Preferred timing (before doors or line check only)
- Full band vs. line check requirements

**Performance:**
- Set length
- Curfew time
- Encore expectations

**Load-Out:**
- Expected duration
- Departure time
- Overnight parking needs

### Personnel Requirements

**Crew:**
- Number of stagehands needed
- Experience level required
- Timing of crew call

**Technical Staff:**
- Sound engineer (house or touring)
- Monitor engineer (if separate)
- Lighting director (house or touring)
- Backline technician (if needed)

**Security:**
- Stage security requirements
- Dressing room security
- Merchandise area security
- Guest list management

### Parking and Transportation

**Vehicle Parking:**
- Number of vehicles
- Size requirements (bus, trailer, van)
- Overnight parking needs
- Loading zone access

**Local Transportation:**
- Runner vehicle needs
- Airport transfer requirements
- Hotel shuttle needs

## Creating Effective Riders

### General Principles

**1. Be Specific but Flexible**
- Specify what you need
- Note acceptable alternatives
- Prioritize requirements (must have vs. nice to have)

**2. Be Realistic**
- Match requests to venue level
- Consider venue budgets
- Don't request what you won't use

**3. Be Professional**
- Clear, organized formatting
- Professional language
- Contact information for questions

**4. Be Current**
- Update riders regularly
- Remove outdated requirements
- Adjust for different venue sizes

### Rider Organization

**Standard Structure:**

```
[ARTIST NAME] - RIDER

CONTACT INFORMATION
Tour Manager: [Name] - [Phone] - [Email]
Production Manager: [Name] - [Phone] - [Email]

1. SCHEDULE
   [Load-in, soundcheck, performance times]

2. STAGE PLOT
   [Diagram attached]

3. INPUT LIST
   [Channel list]

4. AUDIO REQUIREMENTS
   [Console, monitors, mics, etc.]

5. LIGHTING REQUIREMENTS
   [Fixture count, console, effects]

6. POWER REQUIREMENTS
   [Amperage, distribution]

7. DRESSING ROOMS
   [Number, amenities]

8. CATERING
   [Meals, beverages, timing]

9. PARKING
   [Vehicle requirements]

10. MERCHANDISE
    [Space, percentage, staffing]
```

### Version Control

**Multiple Rider Versions:**
- **Club Rider:** 200-500 capacity
- **Theater Rider:** 500-2,000 capacity
- **Arena Rider:** 2,000+ capacity
- **Festival Rider:** Special requirements

**Document Naming:**
```
[ArtistName]_TechRider_Club_2025.pdf
[ArtistName]_HospitalityRider_2025.pdf
[ArtistName]_StagePlot_2025.pdf
```

## Rider Negotiation

### What Can Be Negotiated

**Flexible Items:**
- Specific microphone models
- Catering details
- Timing (within reason)
- Some personnel requests

**Non-Negotiable Items:**
- Safety requirements
- Essential technical needs
- Contractual obligations
- Accessibility requirements

### Handling Conflicts

**When Venues Can't Fulfill Requests:**

1. **Prioritize:** What actually affects the show?
2. **Find alternatives:** Different equipment, adjusted timing
3. **Bring your own:** Consider touring with critical items
4. **Compensate:** Adjust guarantee if venue saves money
5. **Document:** Note any changes for future reference

**Common Conflicts:**
- Console doesn't have enough channels
- No monitor engineer available
- Catering budget limited
- Loading dock too small

## Key Takeaways

1. **Riders protect everyone** — Clear expectations prevent disputes
2. **Be realistic** — Match requests to venue level and budget
3. **Prioritize** — Distinguish needs from wants
4. **Stay current** — Update riders as your production evolves
5. **Communicate** — Include contact info for questions
6. **Be flexible** — Adapt when venues can't fulfill requests
7. **Remember the relationship** — Venues talk; be professional
