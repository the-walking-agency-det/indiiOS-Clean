# Marketing & Promotion: Complete Guide

## Overview

Marketing and promotion are the engines that transform great music into a sustainable career. This guide covers the essential strategies independent artists need to build audiences, generate streams, and create revenue opportunities in today's music industry.

---

## The Modern Marketing Mix

### The Seven Pillars

1. **Playlist Strategy** — Streaming discovery and algorithmic growth
2. **Social Media** — Content creation and community building
3. **Press & PR** — Credibility and third-party validation
4. **Radio Promotion** — Traditional reach and industry credibility
5. **Music Videos** — Visual storytelling and content assets
6. **Brand Partnerships** — Alternative revenue and audience expansion
7. **Email Marketing** — Direct fan relationships you own

---

## The Release Timeline

### The 12-Week Campaign Cycle

| Week | Activity | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| -12 | Announcement | Tease the project, build anticipation |
| -10 | Lead Single | Release with video, pitch to playlists |
| -8 | Press Push | Interviews, features, reviews |
| -6 | Second Single | Maintain momentum |
| -4 | Pre-save Campaign | Drive day-one streams |
| -2 | Final Push | Heavy content, influencer outreach |
| 0 | Release Day | All channels activated |
| +2 | Follow-up Content | Behind-the-scenes, remixes |
| +4 | Sustained Promotion | Keep the algorithm fed |

## Key Principles

### 1. Start Early
Marketing begins before the music is finished. The most successful campaigns have 8–12 weeks of runway.

### 2. Own Your Audience
Social platforms can change algorithms or disappear. Your email list is the only audience you truly own.

### 3. Content Is Fuel
Every release needs 10–20 pieces of supporting content. Plan this in advance.

### 4. Consistency Beats Virality
One viral moment is luck. Consistent growth is strategy. Show up every day.

### 5. Data Drives Decisions
Track what works. Double down on winning channels. Kill underperformers quickly.

## The TikTok-First Strategy

For unknown artists, TikTok is often the most efficient path to discovery:

1. **Post 3–5x daily** during campaign periods
2. **Use trending sounds** strategically with original content
3. **Hook in 0–3 seconds** — attention spans are microscopic
4. **Engage immediately** — reply to comments within the first hour
5. **Cross-post** to Reels and Shorts with platform-specific edits

### Two Types of Playlists

|-----------|-------------|
| Human-curated | AI-generated |
| Hard to get | Easier to trigger |
| Massive reach | Personalized reach |

## Press & PR Fundamentals

### DIY vs. Professional Publicist

**DIY is right when:**
- Budget is under $3,000
- You're building initial buzz
- You have time to research and pitch
- Local/regional coverage is the goal

**Hire a publicist when:**
- Budget is $5,000+
- National coverage is essential
- You have a compelling story
- Previous releases gained traction

See detailed PR strategies in the dedicated sections.

## Radio Promotion

### The Three Tiers

1. **College Radio** — Accessible, builds story, low cost
2. **Satellite Radio** — SiriusXM, niche shows, moderate cost
3. **Terrestrial Radio** — Major markets, expensive, requires promoter

### When to Hire a Radio Promoter

- You have $10,000+ budget
- Song has streaming momentum
- Previous releases performed at radio
- You're targeting major markets

Radio promoters typically charge $3,000–$15,000 per campaign but have relationships that open doors.

## Music Video ROI

### Budget Tiers

| Tier | Budget | Best For |
|------|--------|----------|
| DIY | $0–$1,000 | Lyric videos, performance footage |
| Indie | $1,000–$10,000 | Simple concepts, local crew |
| Professional | $10,000–$50,000 | Full production, known directors |
| Major | $50,000+ | Label-level quality |

### Distribution Strategy
- YouTube (primary)
- Vevo (if eligible)
- Instagram/TikTok clips
- Facebook/Apple Music

The video is content for months. Extract 10–30 short clips from every video.

## Email Marketing

### The Foundation

Your email list is your most valuable marketing asset:

- **Average open rate:** 20–25% (vs. 5% social reach)
- **Conversion rate:** 3–5x higher than social
- **Ownership:** You control the relationship

### List Building Tactics
- Website signup incentives (free download, exclusive content)
- QR codes at shows
- Social media lead magnets
- Pre-save campaigns with email capture
- Contest entries

### Segmentation Strategy
- Super fans (merch buyers, frequent openers)
- Local fans (by city for tour announcements)
- Casual listeners (general updates)
- Industry contacts (press, playlisters, labels)

## Measuring Success

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| Metric | Tool | Target (Single) |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| Streams (first week) | Spotify for Artists | 10,000–100,000 |
| Playlist adds | Chartmetric/Soundcharts | 50+ playlists |
| Social engagement | Native analytics | 5%+ engagement rate |
| Email signups | Mailchimp/ConvertKit | 100–500 |
| Press mentions | Google Alerts | 5–20 articles |
| Video views | YouTube Analytics | 5,000–50,000 |

### Attribution
Use UTM parameters on all links to track which channels drive results. Tools like Linkfire or ToneDen provide consolidated analytics.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Starting too late** — 2 weeks isn't enough for a proper campaign
2. **Ignoring the algorithm** — Social platforms reward consistency
3. **One-and-done posting** — Content needs repetition across platforms
4. **Neglecting email** — Social reach keeps declining
5. **Poor targeting** — Don't spend money reaching the wrong audience
6. **No follow-up** — The week after release matters as much as release week
