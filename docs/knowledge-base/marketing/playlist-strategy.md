# Playlist Strategy: The Complete Guide

## Understanding the Playlist Ecosystem

Streaming playlists are the modern radio — they're how listeners discover new music and how artists build audiences. Understanding how playlists work is essential for any release strategy.

---

## Types of Playlists

### 1. Editorial Playlists

**What they are:** Human-curated playlists by streaming platform employees

**Examples:**
- Spotify: RapCaviar, Today's Top Hits, Indie Pop
- Apple Music: New Music Daily, A-List Pop
- Amazon Music: Breakthrough, Fresh Hip-Hop

**Characteristics:**
- Hand-selected by curators
- Massive follower counts (100K–5M+)
- High prestige and credibility
- Extremely competitive
- Can add 100K–1M+ streams overnight

**How to get on:**
- Build streaming momentum first
- Pitch via Spotify for Artists (3 weeks before release)
- Have a compelling story and brand
- Previous playlist history helps
- Professional presentation matters

### 2. Algorithmic Playlists

**What they are:** AI-generated playlists personalized for each listener

**Examples:**
- Spotify: Discover Weekly, Release Radar, Daily Mix
- Apple Music: New Music Mix, Favorites Mix
- YouTube Music: Discover Mix, New Release Mix

**Characteristics:**
- Personalized to each user
- Based on listening history and behavior
- Updated regularly (weekly/daily)
- Easier to trigger than editorial
- Highly engaged listeners

**How to trigger:**
- Drive saves and follows
- Generate playlist adds from user playlists
- Maintain consistent release schedule
- Encourage repeat listens
- Cross-promote to existing fans

### 3. User-Curated Playlists

**What they are:** Playlists created by listeners, influencers, and brands

**Examples:**
- Influencer playlists (study beats, workout)
- Brand playlists (Nike, Starbucks)
- Fan playlists (genre-specific, mood-based)
- Blogger/critic playlists

**Characteristics:**
- Wide range in quality and reach
- Often more accessible than editorial
- Can drive significant streams
- Good for algorithmic triggers
- Build playlist-to-playlist momentum

**How to get on:**
- Research relevant playlist curators
- Personalized pitching
- Build relationships over time
- Offer value (exclusive content, interviews)
- Use submission platforms (SubmitHub, Groover)

### 4. Personalized Playlists

**What they are:** Hybrid playlists combining editorial and algorithmic elements

**Examples:**
- Spotify: On Repeat, Repeat Rewind
- Apple Music: Get Up! Mix, Chill Mix

**Characteristics:**
- Based on individual listening patterns
- Mix of familiar and new music
- High engagement rates
- Automatic inclusion based on behavior

## The Path to Editorial Playlists

### The Ladder Approach

You don't start at the top. Editorial playlists follow a progression:

```
User Playlists → Algorithmic Playlists → Third-Party Playlists → Editorial Playlists
```

### Step-by-Step Progression

**Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–2)**
- Release and distribute
- Pitch to user-curated playlists
- Drive saves and follows
- Post consistent content
- Goal: 1,000–5,000 streams, 50+ playlist adds

**Phase 2: Algorithmic Trigger (Weeks 3–4)**
- Release Radar inclusion (automatic for followers)
- Discover Weekly consideration
- Daily Mix potential
- Goal: 10,000+ streams, strong save rate

**Phase 3: Momentum Building (Weeks 5–8)**
- Third-party playlist placements
- Press coverage
- Social momentum
- Radio consideration
- Goal: 50,000+ streams, editorial radar

**Phase 4: Editorial Push (Weeks 9–12)**
- Spotify for Artists pitch (if not done)
- Direct curator outreach
- Major playlist consideration
- Goal: Editorial placement, 100K+ streams

## Spotify for Artists: The Pitch Tool

### How to Pitch

1. **Timing:** Submit 3 weeks before release date
2. **Location:** Spotify for Artists → Music → Upcoming
3. **Information to provide:**
   - Song details (mood, genre, instruments)
   - Release details (is it a single? part of EP?)
   - Marketing plans (touring, press, promotion)
   - Story/context (what's the song about?)

### Pitch Best Practices

**Do:**
- Be specific about genre and mood
- Mention similar artists accurately
- Highlight marketing plans
- Tell the story behind the song
- Update with momentum (streams, press)

**Don't:**
- Exaggerate or misrepresent
- Use all caps or excessive punctuation
- Pitch covers or non-original music
- Submit multiple times for same song
- Wait until last minute

### What Happens After Pitching

- Curators review pitches (no timeline guaranteed)
- Songs may be added to various playlists
- You'll receive notification if placed
- Placement can happen weeks or months after release
- One song can be on multiple playlists

## Algorithmic Playlist Optimization

### The Signals That Matter

**Primary Signals:**
1. **Saves** — Most important; indicates strong intent
2. **Playlist Adds** — Shows organic interest
3. **Repeat Listens** — Engagement quality
4. **Share Rate** — Social proof
5. **Follows** — Long-term interest

**Secondary Signals:**
- Skip rate (lower is better)
- Listen-through rate
- Time of day listening
- Device type (mobile vs. desktop)
- Playlist context (what songs surround yours)

### Optimization Strategies

**Drive Saves:**
- Ask fans directly ("Hit that + button")
- Include in social content
- Email campaigns with save CTAs
- Incentivize ("Save for a chance to...")

**Generate Playlist Adds:**
- Create and share your own playlists
- Encourage fan playlists
- Submit to user-curated playlists
- Collaborate with other artists

**Improve Engagement:**
- Strong intros (first 30 seconds critical)
- Song length optimization (2:30–3:30 ideal)
- Quality production (professional mastering)
- Compelling artwork

## Third-Party Playlist Strategy

### Finding Relevant Playlists

**Research Methods:**
1. **Spotify Search:** Genre + "playlist" + activity/mood
2. **Artist.fm:** See where similar artists are playlisted
3. **Chartmetric:** Playlist database and curator contacts
4. **Manual Research:** Check artists at your level

**Playlist Quality Indicators:**
- Follower count (but not the only metric)
- Update frequency (active curation)
- Listener engagement (streams vs. followers)
- Curator legitimacy (real person/brand)
- Playlist focus (consistent theme)

### Pitching User-Curators

**The Outreach Template:**

```
Subject: [Genre] Submission — [Artist Name] "[Song Title]"

Hi [Curator Name],

I came across your [Playlist Name] playlist and really connect with 
the curation—especially your inclusion of [Similar Artist].

I'm releasing "[Song Title]" on [Date], a [genre] track that 
[one-sentence description]. I think it would fit well alongside 
[artist] and [artist] on your playlist.

[Optional: one sentence about momentum—streams, press, etc.]

Spotify pre-save: [link]
Private link: [link]
Press kit: [link]

Thanks for considering,
[Your Name]
[Artist Name]
[Website/Social]
```

### Submission Platforms

| Platform | Cost | Best For |
|----------|------|----------|
| SubmitHub | $1–$3/submission | Blogs, playlists, influencers |
| Groover | €2–€4/submission | European curators, labels |
| Musosoup | Subscription | Blogs, playlists, radio |
| Playlist Push | $300+/campaign | Guaranteed placements |
| Daily Playlists | Free tier | Entry-level playlist access |

**Platform Tips:**
- Start with free/low-cost options
- Target curators who feature similar artists
- Read curator guidelines carefully
- Track results and double down on what works
- Build relationships beyond transactions

## Measuring Playlist Success

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Tool | Good | Great |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| Total Playlist Adds | Spotify for Artists | 50+ | 200+ |
| Editorial Playlists | Spotify for Artists | 1 | 3+ |
| Algorithmic Reach | Spotify for Artists | 20% | 50%+ |
| Playlist Followers | Chartmetric | 100K | 1M+ |
| Save Rate | Spotify for Artists | 3% | 8%+ |

### Playlist Analytics Tools

- **Spotify for Artists:** Free, essential data
- **Chartmetric:** Comprehensive playlist tracking ($)
- **Soundcharts:** Playlist and social analytics ($)
- **Forerunner:** Playlist and trend analysis ($)
- **Artist.fm:** Free playlist tracking

### Red Flags

- Playlist with 100K followers but 1K monthly listeners
- Sudden follower spikes (bot playlists)
- No curator information or contact
- Generic playlist names with no focus
- Promises of guaranteed streams

## Common Mistakes

1. **Pitching too late** — Spotify requires 3 weeks minimum
2. **Ignoring algorithmic playlists** — They're easier and highly engaged
3. **Paying for guaranteed editorial** — No one can guarantee editorial placement
4. **Bot playlist schemes** — Fake streams hurt your algorithmic standing
5. **One-size-fits-all pitching** — Personalization matters
6. **Neglecting user playlists** — They're the path to editorial
7. **Giving up too soon** — Playlist success can take months

## Advanced Strategies

### The Playlist Flywheel

```
User Playlist Adds → Algorithmic Playlists → More Streams → 
More Playlist Adds → Editorial Consideration → Editorial Placement → 
Massive Streams → More Algorithmic Love
```

### Collaborative Playlists

- Partner with other artists for mutual playlist placement
- Create shared playlists with complementary artists
- Feature artists who might feature you back

### Playlist Seeding

- Release multiple versions (radio edit, acoustic, remix)
- Each version is a new playlist opportunity
- Stagger releases to maintain momentum

### International Expansion

- Target country-specific playlists after US/Canada success
- Consider language versions for global markets
- Use distributor's international pitching services
