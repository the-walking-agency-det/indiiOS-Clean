# Music Industry Contracts & Agreements

## Overview

Contracts are the foundation of every professional relationship in the music industry. Whether you're a producer hiring a vocalist, a band formalizing your partnership, or an artist signing with management, the contract defines who owns what, who gets paid how much, and what happens when things go wrong.

**This guide covers the essential agreements every music professional needs to understand.**

---

## Key Concepts

### Work-for-Hire vs. Collaboration

| Aspect | Work-for-Hire | Collaboration |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Ownership** | Hiring party owns 100% | Shared ownership |
| **Payment** | One-time fee | Royalties/ongoing revenue |
| **Credit** | May or may not be credited | Typically credited |
| **Creative Control** | Hiring party decides | Joint decisions |
| **Future Use** | No additional compensation | Ongoing royalty payments |

**Critical Rule:** Work-for-hire means **they own it, you get paid once.** You cannot reclaim rights later, even if the song becomes a hit.

### Points on a Record

"Points" refer to percentage points of the retail or wholesale price of a record that go to the producer as royalties.

- **New producers:** 1-3 points
- **Established producers:** 3-5 points
- **Superstar producers:** 5-7+ points

Points are calculated from the suggested retail list price (SRLP) or published price to dealers (PPD), not the full retail price.

### Commission Structures

| Role | Standard Commission | Notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| **Personal Manager** | 15-20% of gross income | Sometimes 10% for major touring income |
| **Booking Agent** | 10% of performance fees | Capped by union regulations |
| **Business Manager** | 5% of gross income | Often replaced by flat fee for high earners |
| **Publicist** | $2,000-10,000/month retainer | Or per-campaign fees |

### What Makes a Contract Enforceable

1. **Offer and Acceptance** — Clear terms agreed upon by all parties
2. **Consideration** — Something of value exchanged (money, services, rights)
3. **Capacity** — All parties legally able to enter contracts
4. **Legality** — Purpose must be legal
5. **Written Form** — Copyright assignments must be in writing (US Copyright Act)

**Verbal agreements in music are worth the paper they're written on.**

## Types of Agreements

### 1. Producer Agreements

Producer agreements define the relationship between artists and producers, covering creative control, compensation, and ownership.

**Key Elements:**
- Recording budget and delivery schedule
- Producer fee (upfront payment)
- Royalty points (typically 3-5%)
- Creative approval rights
- Credit requirements
- Work-for-hire vs. collaboration status

[→ Deep dive into Producer Agreements](./producer-agreements.md)

### 2. Featured Artist Agreements

When an artist appears on another artist's track, featured artist agreements prevent disputes over splits, credits, and approvals.

**Key Elements:**
- Revenue split (often 50/50 or negotiated percentage)
- Credit placement (featuring vs. with vs. and)
- Approval rights over final mix
- Marketing obligations
- Clearances for samples used

### 3. Work-for-Hire Agreements

Used when you want full ownership of creative work without ongoing royalty obligations.

**Common Uses:**
- Hiring session musicians
- Commissioning artwork or photography
- Ghostwriting services
- Work-made-for-hire under US Copyright Act

**What You Lose as a Work-for-Hire:**
- Copyright ownership
- Future royalty income
- Moral rights (in some jurisdictions)
- Ability to reclaim rights after 35 years (termination rights)

### 4. Band Partnership Agreements

Essential for any group operating as a unit. Prevents the "no contract, no problem until there's a problem" scenario.

**Key Elements:**
- Ownership structure (LLC vs. partnership)
- Revenue splits (equal vs. contribution-based)
- Decision-making procedures
- Band name ownership
- Exit/buyout provisions
- Voting rights on major decisions

[→ Deep dive into Band Partnerships](./band-partnerships.md)

### 5. Management Contracts

Management agreements are long-term relationships that can make or break careers. They're also notoriously difficult to exit.

**Key Elements:**
- Term length (1-5 years, often with options)
- Commission percentage and scope
- Sunset clause (post-term commissions)
- Key man provision (what if your manager leaves?)
- Expense reimbursement
- Termination for cause vs. convenience

[→ Deep dive into Management Contracts](./management-contracts.md)

### 6. Booking Agent Contracts

Booking agents secure live performance opportunities and are regulated by unions in the US and Canada.

**Key Elements:**
- Territory (geographic scope)
- Exclusivity (can you have multiple agents?)
- Commission rate (typically 10%)
- Radius clauses (can't play competing venues nearby)
- Term and termination
- Union compliance (AFM in US/Canada)

### 7. Session Musician Agreements

Covers hired musicians who perform on recordings without becoming copyright owners.

**Key Elements:**
- Work-for-hire designation
- Session rate (union scale or negotiated)
- Reuse fees (for additional uses)
- Credit (if any)
- Payment terms

## Termination Clauses and Reversion Rights

### Termination for Cause

Either party can terminate if the other:
- Materially breaches the agreement
- Becomes insolvent or bankrupt
- Engages in conduct damaging to reputation
- Fails to cure breach within specified time (usually 30 days)

### Termination for Convenience

Some contracts allow termination without cause, often with:
- Notice period (30-180 days)
- Financial penalties
- Continued obligations for existing projects

### Reversion Rights

Reversion clauses return rights to the original owner under specific conditions:

- **Out of print:** If recording is unavailable for 6-12 months
- **Non-exploitation:** If label doesn't release within specified time
- **Breach:** If label fails to pay royalties
- **Term expiration:** Rights return after contract ends

**US Copyright Act Termination Rights:**
- Authors can terminate grants after 35 years
- Must give 2-10 years advance notice
- Cannot be waived in the original contract
- Applies to work-for-hire only in limited circumstances

## Union Rates and Regulations (US/Canada)

### American Federation of Musicians (AFM)

The AFM sets minimum rates for:
- Recording sessions (scale wages)
- Live performances
- Reuse fees for recordings
- Pension and health contributions

**Key AFM Agreements:**
- **Sound Recording Labor Agreement (SRLA):** Major label sessions
- **Integrated Media Agreement (IMA):** New media, streaming
- **Live Performance:** Venue-specific scales

### SAG-AFTRA

Covers vocalists on sound recordings:
- Minimum session fees
- Royalties for streaming
- Pension and health contributions
- Reuse fees for commercials, film/TV

**Important:** Union status affects:
- Who you can work with
- Where recordings can be used
- Benefit eligibility
- Rate calculations

## Red Flags in Music Contracts

### 🚩 Vague Language
- "Reasonable efforts" without definition
- "Industry standard" without specifics
- Open-ended delivery requirements

### 🚩 One-Sided Terms
- Unlimited indemnification
- No audit rights
- Automatic renewals without consent

### 🚩 Missing Protections
- No reversion clause
- No termination for convenience
- No cap on expenses

### 🚩 Ambiguous Payment Terms
- "Net profits" without definition
- Recoupment of unspecified costs
- Delayed royalty payments without interest

### 🚩 Overreaching Rights
- Life-of-copyright grants
- Worldwide rights when unnecessary
- Broad merchandising without additional compensation

## Best Practices

### Before Signing Anything

1. **Read every word** — Don't skim; contracts are written to be enforced as written
2. **Get it reviewed** — Entertainment lawyer fees ($300-800/hr) are cheaper than litigation
3. **Understand the math** — Calculate actual dollar amounts, not just percentages
4. **Negotiate** — Everything is negotiable; silence means acceptance
5. **Get it in writing** — Verbal promises mean nothing

### During the Relationship

1. **Document everything** — Save emails, texts, meeting notes
2. **Track payments** — Maintain your own royalty records
3. **Communicate in writing** — Follow up verbal agreements with email confirmation
4. **Know your deadlines** — Option periods, cure periods, notice requirements

### When Things Go Wrong

1. **Review the contract** — Know your rights and obligations
2. **Document the breach** — Gather evidence of violations
3. **Send formal notice** — Follow contract procedures for disputes
4. **Consider mediation** — Cheaper and faster than litigation
5. **Consult an attorney** — Before taking action that could be seen as breach

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Advance** | Upfront payment recouped from future royalties |
| **All-in royalty** | Rate that includes both artist and producer share |
| **Cross-collateralization** | Using earnings from one project to cover losses on another |
| **Escrow** | Third-party holding of funds until conditions are met |
| **Indemnification** | Promise to cover losses if claims arise |
| **Mechanical royalty** | Payment for reproducing/compressing musical compositions |
| **Option period** | Timeframe for exercising contract extensions |
| **PPD** | Published Price to Dealers (wholesale price) |
| **Recoupment** | Process of earning back advances before royalties are paid |
| **SRLP** | Suggested Retail List Price (sticker price) |
| **Sunset clause** | Post-contract commission period for managers |
