# Band Partnership Agreements: Managing Group Dynamics

## Why Bands Need Partnership Agreements

A band partnership agreement is a contract between band members that defines how the group operates, makes decisions, splits money, and handles departures. Without one, you're operating on assumptions that will eventually conflict.

**The hard truth:** Most bands break up. A good partnership agreement ensures the breakup doesn't destroy relationships, finances, or the ability to continue making music.

---

## The "No Contract" Problem

### What Happens Without an Agreement

**Scenario 1: The Hit Song Dispute**
- Band writes a hit, one member contributed the main riff
- That member claims 50% of songwriting, others want equal split
- Lawsuit ensues, band breaks up, no one can use the name

**Scenario 2: The Departure**
- Founding member quits to go solo
- Who owns the band name? The songs? The social media accounts?
- Remaining members can't tour; departing member can't use the brand

**Scenario 3: The Uneven Contribution**
- One member writes all the songs, others just perform
- Songwriter wants publishing royalties + equal performance split
- Other members feel they contributed to the sound
- Resentment builds, band implodes

### Legal Defaults (What Happens If You Don't Decide)

**General Partnership (Default):**
- Equal ownership and voting rights
- Equal share of profits and losses
- Each partner can bind the partnership to contracts
- Personal liability for partnership debts
- Dissolves if any member leaves

**This is rarely what bands actually want.**

## Choosing a Business Structure

### 1. General Partnership (Simplest)

**Pros:**
- No formation paperwork required
- Pass-through taxation
- Equal say by default

**Cons:**
- Unlimited personal liability
- Any member can bind the group
- Dissolves on member departure
- No clear exit mechanism

**Best For:** Hobby bands with minimal income/assets

### 2. Limited Liability Company (LLC) (Recommended)

**Pros:**
- Limited liability protection
- Flexible profit/loss allocation
- Continues despite member changes
- Clear operating agreement structure
- Pass-through taxation

**Cons:**
- Formation costs ($100-1,000 depending on state/province)
- Annual fees in some jurisdictions
- More paperwork than partnership

**Best For:** Working bands with income, assets, or liability exposure

### 3. Corporation (Rare for Bands)

**Pros:**
- Maximum liability protection
- Easier to bring in investors
- Perpetual existence

**Cons:**
- Double taxation (C-corp) or restrictions (S-corp)
- Complex formation and maintenance
- Overkill for most bands

**Best For:** Major touring acts with significant business operations

## Key Provisions in Band Partnership Agreements

### 1. Ownership Structure

**Who Owns What:**

| Asset Type | Ownership Options |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Band name** | Equal / Senior members only / LLC owns |
| **Sound recordings** | Equal / Contribution-based / LLC owns |
| **Musical compositions** | Songwriter(s) / Equal / Split by contribution |
| **Equipment** | Individual / Partnership / LLC |
| **Merchandise designs** | Designer / Equal / LLC |
| **Social media/website** | Equal / Designated member / LLC |

**Voting Rights:**
- Equal votes (1 per member)
- Weighted by contribution
- Senior members have tie-breaker
- Unanimous for major decisions, majority for routine

**Decision Categories:**

| Decision Type | Typical Voting Requirement |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Day-to-day operations** | Majority |
| **Hiring/firing members** | Unanimous or supermajority |
| **Major expenses** | Unanimous or supermajority |
| **Recording/signing deals** | Unanimous |
| **Licensing/synch** | Unanimous or designated member |
| **Dissolution** | Unanimous or high threshold |

### 2. Revenue Splits

**Performance Income (Gigs, Touring):**

| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Equal split** | All members get same percentage | Bands with equal contribution |
| **Equal after expenses** | Split net, not gross | Bands with significant touring costs |
| **Base + bonus** | Equal base, extra for bandleader | Hierarchical bands |
| **Per diem + equal split** | Daily rate plus equal share | Touring-focused bands |

**Recording Income (Royalties, Sales):**

| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Equal split** | All members share equally | Collaborative writing |
| **Songwriter split** | Writers get publishing, band splits masters | Mixed writing contribution |
| **Contribution-based** | Percentage based on input | Uneven creative roles |
| **Side musician rate** | Non-writers get flat fee or lower percentage | Clear leader + backing |

**Merchandise Income:**
- Usually split equally (band-branded items)
- Individual members keep solo merch revenue
- Designer may get additional percentage

**Publishing Income (Songwriting):**

**Critical distinction:** Publishing is separate from performance/recording income.

| Scenario | Typical Split |
|----------|---------------|
| **All members write equally** | Equal publishing split |
| **Primary songwriter + contributors** | 50% primary, 50% split among contributors |
| **One main writer, others arrange** | Writer keeps publishing, others get performance royalties |
| **External co-writers** | As negotiated with co-writer |

### 3. Member Roles and Responsibilities

**Define Each Member's Role:**

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Bandleader** | Final decisions, press spokesperson, deal negotiations |
| **Musical director** | Arrangements, setlists, rehearsals |
| **Business manager** | Finances, bookkeeping, tax compliance |
| **Tour manager** (if member) | Logistics, travel, day-of-show operations |
| **Creative director** | Visuals, branding, merchandise design |

**Time Commitments:**
- Minimum rehearsal requirements
- Availability for gigs/recording
- Notice requirements for missing commitments
- Consequences for non-attendance

### 4. Adding New Members

**Process for Adding Members:**

1. **Unanimous vote** (typical)
2. **Trial period** (30-90 days)
3. **Formal vote** for permanent status
4. **Amend partnership agreement** to include new member
5. **Buy-in** (if applicable) for existing assets

**New Member Considerations:**
- Do they get retroactive ownership of existing songs?
- What percentage of future income?
- Do they have voting rights immediately?
- What happens to their share if they leave?

### 5. Departure and Exit Clauses

**Types of Departure:**

| Type | Definition | Typical Treatment |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Voluntary departure** | Member chooses to leave | Buyout at fair market value |
| **Involuntary removal** | Band votes to remove | For cause, minimal or no payout |
| **Death/disability** | Member unable to continue | Estate retains rights or buyout |
| **Breach** | Violation of agreement | Forfeiture of rights possible |

**Buyout Mechanisms:**

**For Band Name:**
- Departing member cannot use name
- Remaining members continue with name
- Valuation based on goodwill, recognition

**For Recordings/Songs:**
- Departing member retains share of existing works
- Or band buys out at agreed valuation
- Future works: no share unless contributed

**For Equipment:**
- Return contributed equipment
- Buyout at depreciated value
- Or sell and split proceeds

**Valuation Methods:**
- Appraisal by neutral third party
- Formula based on revenue (e.g., 2x annual earnings)
- Pre-agreed schedule in partnership agreement
- Right of first refusal for remaining members

### 6. Band Name Ownership

**The Most Contested Asset**

**Ownership Options:**

| Model | Description | Risk Level |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Equal ownership** | All members own name jointly | High (departing members can block use) |
| **Senior members** | Founders own name | Medium (creates hierarchy) |
| **LLC owns** | Business entity owns name | Low (cleanest structure) |
| **Individual + license** | One member owns, licenses to band | Low for owner, high for others |

**Trademark Protection:**
- Register band name as trademark
- Prevents other bands from using same name
- Strengthens claim in disputes
- Costs $250-750 per class (USPTO)

**Name Use After Departure:**
- Departing member typically cannot use name
- May use "formerly of [Band Name]"
- May not form competing band with similar name

### 7. Decision-Making Procedures

**Majority vs. Unanimous Decisions:**

**Require Unanimous Vote:**
- Signing recording contracts
- Major financial commitments
- Adding/removing members
- Dissolving the partnership
- Licensing deals over $X

**Majority Vote Sufficient:**
- Routine gig bookings
- Minor equipment purchases
- Rehearsal scheduling
- Social media posts

**Tie-Breaking Mechanisms:**
- Bandleader has tie-breaker vote
- Rotate tie-breaker by year
- External advisor decides
- No action without consensus

### 8. Financial Management

**Banking:**
- Partnership/LLC bank account required
- Signature authority (2+ members typical)
- No personal use of band funds

**Accounting:**
- Monthly financial statements
- Annual review by all members
- Right to audit books
- Tax preparation and filing

**Expense Categories:**

| Category | Typical Treatment |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Recording costs** | Band expense, recouped from royalties |
| **Touring costs** | Deducted from tour income |
| **Equipment** | Band asset or individual depending |
| **Marketing/promo** | Band expense |
| **Legal/accounting** | Band expense |
| **Individual lessons** | Individual expense |

## Sample Scenarios and Solutions

### Scenario 1: The Songwriting Dispute

**Situation:**
- 4-piece band, everyone contributes to sound
- Guitarist writes all lyrics and melodies
- Others argue their arrangements are creative contributions

**Solutions:**

| Approach | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Pure songwriter** | Guitarist keeps 100% publishing, band splits performance income |
| **Collaborative** | Equal publishing split, acknowledging everyone's contribution |
| **Hybrid** | Guitarist gets 50%, rest split 50% among arrangers |
| **Future-only** | Past songs: guitarist owns. Future songs: equal split going forward |

**Prevention:** Define publishing splits in writing before writing together.

### Scenario 2: The Founding Member Departure

**Situation:**
- Drummer founded band, recruited other members
- Drummer wants to leave, claims ownership of band name
- Other members want to continue

**Solutions:**

| Agreement Provision | Outcome |
|---------------------|---------|
| **No provision** | Legal battle, name likely unusable by anyone |
| **Equal ownership** | Drummer can block use or demand buyout |
| **LLC owns name** | Band continues, departing member gets LLC share value |
| **Founder retains** | Drummer keeps name, others start new band |

**Best Practice:** Band name owned by LLC, not individuals.

### Scenario 3: The Uneven Time Commitment

**Situation:**
- Keyboard player has day job, misses rehearsals
- Other members frustrated but don't want to replace them

**Solutions:**

| Approach | Implementation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Minimum requirements** | Contract specifies rehearsal attendance (e.g., 80%) |
| **Graduated consequences** | Warning → reduced pay → suspension → removal |
| **Buyout option** | Band can buy out non-performing member |
| **Reduced share** | Lower percentage for lower commitment |

## Red Flags in Band Partnerships

### 🚩 Vague Ownership Language
- "We all own everything" — Own what, exactly?
- No distinction between songwriting and recording
- No clarity on band name ownership

### 🚩 No Exit Mechanism
- What happens when someone leaves?
- How is value determined?
- Can departing member block future use?

### 🚩 Equal Splits Regardless of Contribution
- May seem fair initially
- Creates resentment when contributions diverge
- Hard to change once established

### 🚩 No Decision-Making Process
- "We decide together" — How?
- What if you disagree?
- Who breaks ties?

### 🚩 Ignoring Publishing
- Focusing only on performance income
- Publishing often worth more long-term
- Songwriting credits determine publishing splits

### 🚩 No Dispute Resolution
- Going straight to litigation
- No mediation or arbitration clause
- Expensive and relationship-destroying

## Best Practices for Bands

### When Forming

1. **Write it down immediately** — Don't wait for success
2. **Address the hard questions** — What if someone quits? Dies? Gets fired?
3. **Be specific** — "Fair" means different things to different people
4. **Plan for success** — Today's hobby might be tomorrow's career
5. **Get legal help** — Band agreement costs $1,000-5,000; disputes cost $50,000+

### During the Relationship

1. **Hold regular business meetings** — Monthly or quarterly
2. **Review finances together** — Transparency prevents suspicion
3. **Document decisions** — Email confirmations after verbal agreements
4. **Amend as needed** — Update agreement when circumstances change
5. **Address issues early** — Small resentments become big problems

### When Problems Arise

1. **Review the agreement** — What does it say?
2. **Try mediation** — Neutral third party can help
3. **Consult attorneys** — Each member should have independent counsel
4. **Consider the relationship** — Legal victory might destroy friendship
5. **Document everything** — If litigation happens, you'll need evidence

## Sample Band Partnership Agreement Outline

```
BAND PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

1. PARTIES AND FORMATION
   - Band name and members
   - Formation date and jurisdiction
   - Business structure (partnership/LLC)

2. PURPOSE
   - Musical performance and recording
   - Related business activities

3. CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS
   - Initial contributions (equipment, money)
   - Future contribution requirements

4. OWNERSHIP OF ASSETS
   - Band name (who owns, use restrictions)
   - Sound recordings (ownership splits)
   - Musical compositions (publishing splits)
   - Equipment (individual vs. partnership)
   - Intellectual property (logos, artwork)

5. ALLOCATION OF PROFITS AND LOSSES
   - Performance income splits
   - Recording royalty splits
   - Publishing income splits
   - Merchandise splits
   - Expense allocation

6. MANAGEMENT AND DECISIONS
   - Member roles and responsibilities
   - Voting procedures
   - Unanimous vs. majority decisions
   - Tie-breaking mechanisms

7. MEMBERSHIP CHANGES
   - Adding new members (process, vote)
   - Voluntary departure (buyout, notice)
   - Involuntary removal (cause, process)
   - Death/disability (estate rights)

8. DISSOLUTION
   - Events triggering dissolution
   - Asset liquidation process
   - Name use post-dissolution

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
   - Mediation requirement
   - Arbitration provisions
   - Governing law

10. MISCELLANEOUS
    - Amendment procedures
    - Assignment restrictions
    - Entire agreement clause
```

## US vs. Canada Considerations

### United States

**State Law Variations:**
- Partnership law varies by state
- LLC formation and fees vary significantly
- Community property states affect spousal rights

**Tax Considerations:**
- Pass-through taxation for partnerships/LLCs
- Self-employment tax on partnership income
- Quarterly estimated tax payments required

### Canada

**Business Structures:**
- Partnership (general or limited)
- Corporation (federal or provincial)
- Sole proprietorship (not recommended for bands)

**Tax Considerations:**
- GST/HST registration required over $30,000
- Partnership income flows to personal returns
- Canadian-controlled private corporation (CCPC) advantages

**SOCAN Considerations:**
- Performance rights organization
- Affects publishing income collection
- Membership requirements for songwriters
