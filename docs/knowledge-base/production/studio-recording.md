# Studio Recording Guide

## Overview

Studio recording is the foundation of professional music production. This guide covers everything from booking your first session to understanding contracts and deliverables for independent artists in North America.

---

## Types of Recording Studios

### Home Studios
- **Cost:** $25–$75/hour or flat per-song rates
- **Best for:** Demos, pre-production, vocal tracking, electronic music
- **Pros:** Affordable, flexible scheduling, comfortable environment
- **Cons:** Limited equipment, acoustics may vary, no live room for full bands

### Project Studios
- **Cost:** $50–$150/hour
- **Best for:** Full productions, small bands, professional vocals
- **Pros:** Better gear, treated rooms, experienced engineers
- **Cons:** May lack live room space for large ensembles

### Commercial Studios
- **Cost:** $150–$500+/hour
- **Best for:** Major releases, label projects, orchestral recording
- **Pros:** World-class equipment, multiple rooms, legendary acoustics
- **Cons:** Expensive, booked months in advance

## Booking Process

### Step 1: Research & Contact
- Listen to recent work from the studio/engineer
- Check availability 2–8 weeks in advance
- Ask about engineer experience with your genre

### Step 2: Studio Tour (Recommended)
- Assess the vibe and comfort level
- Check monitoring quality
- Verify equipment list meets your needs

### Step 3: Schedule & Deposit
- Most studios require 50% deposit to book
- Cancellation policies vary (typically 48–72 hours notice)
- Block booking discounts often available (10+ hours)

### Step 4: Pre-Production
- Send demos/reference tracks in advance
- Confirm key/tempo maps
- Prepare lyric sheets and chord charts

## Studio Rates (2024–2025)

### Hourly vs. Day Rates
- **Hourly:** Best for short sessions, overdubs, mixing tweaks
- **Day Rate (8–12 hrs):** 15–25% discount vs. hourly
- **Lockout (24 hrs):** Full control, often used for albums

### Typical Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Hourly | Day Rate | Includes |
|------|--------|----------|----------|
| Budget | $30–$75 | $250–$500 | Basic room, engineer |
| Mid-Level | $75–$150 | $600–$1,200 | Good gear, experienced engineer |
| Premium | $150–$300 | $1,200–$2,400 | High-end mics/converters, top engineer |
| World-Class | $300–$800+ | $2,500–$6,000+ | Legendary rooms, Grammy-winning staff |

### Additional Costs
- **Assistant Engineer:** $150–$300/day
- **Session Musicians:** $100–$500/song (union rates higher)
- **Equipment Rental:** $50–$500/day (specialty mics, outboard)
- **Catering:** $15–$30/person/day
- **Parking:** $10–$40/day (urban studios)

## Studio Contracts

### What to Look For

#### 1. Session Details
- Dates, times, studio room assigned
- Engineer/producer name
- Equipment guarantees

#### 2. Payment Terms
- Deposit amount and due date
- Balance payment schedule
- Overtime rates (typically 1.5x after 10–12 hours)
- Accepted payment methods

#### 3. Cancellation Policy
- Notice required for refund
- Weather/emergency clauses
- Rescheduling fees

#### 4. Deliverables
- File formats provided
- Delivery timeline
- Archive/backup policy

#### 5. Intellectual Property
- Who owns the raw recordings
- Work-for-hire vs. shared ownership
- Credit requirements

### Red Flags
- No written contract
- Vague deliverables
- "We own your masters" clauses
- No backup/archiving mentioned
- Excessive overtime penalties

### Standard Contract Clauses

**Work for Hire:**
> "All recordings made during sessions shall be considered works made for hire. Artist retains all rights to final masters. Studio retains right to use recordings for portfolio/demo purposes unless otherwise specified in writing."

**Payment:**
> "A non-refundable deposit of 50% is required to confirm booking. Balance due at conclusion of final session. Overtime billed at 1.5x standard rate after 10 hours."

**File Delivery:**
> "Studio will deliver final files within 5 business days of final session in format(s) specified: [ ] WAV 24-bit/48kHz [ ] Pro Tools session [ ] Logic session. Raw multitracks available for additional fee of $___/song."

## What to Bring

### Essential
- [ ] Instrument(s) and backup strings/sticks/reeds
- [ ] DI box (if playing electric guitar/bass direct)
- [ ] Headphones (your own for comfort/hygiene)
- [ ] Lyric sheets (printed, large font)
- [ ] Chord charts / lead sheets
- [ ] Water bottle (no ice—reduces vocal strain)
- [ ] Throat coat tea / honey
- [ ] Phone charger

### Recommended
- [ ] Reference tracks (on phone or USB)
- [ ] Click track/tempo map
- [ ] MIDI files for virtual instruments
- [ ] Backup hard drive (for session files)
- [ ] Snacks (avoid dairy before vocals)
- [ ] Comfortable clothes (layers for temperature changes)

### For Vocal Sessions
- [ ] Printed lyrics with verse/chorus markings
- [ ] Warm-up routine completed beforehand
- [ ] Avoid: dairy, caffeine, alcohol 24hrs prior
- [ ] Hydrate heavily day before

## Session Workflow

### Setup (30–60 min)
- Load-in and equipment setup
- Line check and headphone mixes
- Room mic placement and testing

### Tracking
- Record multiple takes (3–5 minimum)
- Keep takes organized with clear labels
- Record "safety" takes after nailing it
- Comp (compile) best parts as you go

### Breaks
- 10 min break every hour (vocal health)
- Ears fatigue—fresh perspective matters
- Review takes away from monitors

### End of Session
- Backup everything to multiple locations
- Confirm file delivery timeline
- Schedule next session if needed

## Deliverables

### Standard Deliverables

| Format | Specs | Use Case |
|--------|-------|----------|
| WAV | 24-bit / 48kHz | Standard delivery for mixing |
| WAV | 24-bit / 96kHz | High-resolution archiving |
| Pro Tools Session | v2022+ | Mix engineer preference |
| Logic Project | v10.7+ | If recorded in Logic |
| Raw Stems | Individual tracks | For mixing elsewhere |

### File Organization
```
ArtistName_SongName_YYYYMMDD/
├── 01_RawStems/
│   ├── Kick.wav
│   ├── Snare.wav
│   ├── BassDI.wav
│   ├── GuitarL.wav
│   ├── GuitarR.wav
│   ├── LeadVox.wav
│   └── BVox1.wav
├── 02_RefMix/
│   └── SongName_RoughMix.wav
├── 03_Sessions/
│   └── SongName.ptx
└── 04_Docs/
    ├── LyricSheet.pdf
    └── TempoMap.txt
```

### What's NOT Included (Usually)
- Mixing (separate service, $200–$2,000/song)
- Mastering (separate service, $50–$500/song)
- Tuning/editing (often $50–$150/song additional)
- Additional file formats (may incur conversion fees)

## Tips for Success

### Before the Session
1. **Rehearse to a click**—saves hours in studio
2. **Pre-produce at home**—know your arrangements
3. **Change strings/heads**—day before, not day of
4. **Sleep well**—fatigue kills performances

### During the Session
1. **Trust your engineer**—they want you to sound good
2. **Don't overthink**—first takes often have the magic
3. **Record everything**—"false starts" sometimes capture gold
4. **Take breaks**—ears and voices need rest

### After the Session
1. **Backup immediately**—studio liability ends after delivery
2. **Listen on multiple systems**—car, earbuds, home stereo
3. **Live with rough mixes**—before committing to final mix
4. **Keep communication open**—with engineer for revisions
