# Catalog Valuation: What Your Songs Are Worth

Understanding what your music catalog is worth is essential for making informed decisions about deals, sales, and your financial future. This guide breaks down how catalogs are valued, what factors matter, and how to calculate your own worth.

---

## Why Catalog Valuation Matters

Knowing your catalog's value helps you:
- Negotiate better publishing deals
- Decide whether to sell vs. keep your catalog
- Secure loans or advances against your catalog
- Plan for retirement (catalogs are assets that generate income)
- Understand if a publisher's offer is fair

---

## The Basics: How Catalogs Are Valued

Catalogs are typically valued as a multiple of their **annual net publisher's share (NPS)** — the actual money the catalog generates after collection fees.

### The Formula

```
Catalog Value = Annual NPS × Multiplier
```

**Example:**
- Your catalog generates $50,000/year in gross publishing royalties
- After collection fees (15%), your NPS is $42,500
- Industry multiplier is 10x
- **Catalog Value = $42,500 × 10 = $425,000**

---

## Understanding Net Publisher's Share (NPS)

### Gross vs. Net

**Gross Publishing Revenue:**
- All money collected from all sources
- Before any fees or splits

**Net Publisher's Share (NPS):**
- Gross minus collection/administration fees
- The actual amount the publisher (or you) receives

### Calculating Your NPS

**If Self-Published with Admin Service:**
```
Gross Royalties: $50,000
Admin Fee (15%): -$7,500
NPS: $42,500
```

**If in Co-Pub Deal:**
```
Gross Royalties: $50,000
Your Share (50% of pub share + 100% writer): $37,500
Admin Fee (if applicable): -$0
NPS: $37,500
```

## The Multiplier: What Drives Value

Industry multipliers typically range from **8x to 18x** annual NPS, depending on:

### 1. Catalog Age and Stability

| Factor | Impact on Multiplier |
|--------|---------------------|
| Established hits (10+ years old) | Higher — proven longevity |
| Recent hits (2-5 years) | Medium — still earning |
| New catalog (<2 years) | Lower — unproven |
| Declining earnings | Lower — negative trend |

**Why:** Buyers want predictable income. A 15-year-old hit that still earns is worth more than a 1-year-old hit that might fade.

### 2. Earnings Trend

| Trend | Typical Multiplier |
|-------|-------------------|
| Growing 20%+ YoY | 14x-18x |
| Stable (±10%) | 10x-14x |
| Declining 10-20% | 8x-10x |
| Declining >20% | 6x-8x |

### 3. Song Diversity

| Catalog Type | Impact |
|--------------|--------|
| One-hit wonder | Lower — concentration risk |
| 5-10 solid songs | Medium — diversified |
| 50+ songs, consistent earners | Higher — stable |
| Deep catalog with classics | Highest — institutional value |

### 4. Genre and Usage

| Type | Typical Premium |
|------|----------------|
| Pop/rock hits | Baseline |
| Hip-hop | High — samples create ongoing value |
| Christmas music | Very high — annual recurrence |
| Film/TV themes | High — sync value |
| Catalog with samples cleared | High — reusable IP |

### 5. Rights Included

| Rights Package | Value Impact |
|----------------|--------------|
| Worldwide, all rights | 100% (baseline) |
| US only | 40-60% |
| Writer's share only | Not typically sold |
| Publisher's share only | 50% of full value |

## Real-World Valuation Examples

### Example 1: The Indie Songwriter

**Catalog:** 30 songs, 3 moderate indie hits
**Annual Earnings:** $15,000 gross
**Deal Type:** Self-published with Songtrust (15% fee)
**NPS:** $12,750

**Valuation:**
- Multiplier: 8x (unproven long-term)
- **Catalog Value: $102,000**

**What this means:**
- A publisher might offer a $50K-$75K advance for a co-pub deal
- Selling outright might fetch $80K-$100K
- Keeping and growing could be worth more long-term

### Example 2: The Established Writer

**Catalog:** 100+ songs, 5 major hits, 10+ album cuts
**Annual Earnings:** $250,000 gross
**Deal Type:** Co-pub (owns 150% of 200%)
**NPS:** $187,500

**Valuation:**
- Multiplier: 12x (proven, stable)
- **Catalog Value: $2,250,000**

**What this means:**
- Could secure a $500K+ advance on a new deal
- Catalog sale could fetch $2M+
- Strong negotiating position

### Example 3: The Legacy Artist

**Catalog:** 200+ songs, multiple platinum records, 20+ years old
**Annual Earnings:** $1,000,000+ gross
**Deal Type:** Full publishing deal
**NPS:** $500,000 (50% of gross)

**Valuation:**
- Multiplier: 15x-18x (institutional asset)
- **Catalog Value: $7,500,000 - $9,000,000**

**What this means:**
- Major financial asset
- Can borrow against catalog value
- Estate planning critical

## Calculating Your Own Catalog Value

### Step 1: Gather Your Data

Collect 3 years of royalty statements:
- PRO statements (ASCAP, BMI, etc.)
- Mechanical collections (The MLC, HFA)
- Sync fees
- Any other publishing income

### Step 2: Calculate Average Annual Gross

```
Year 1: $12,000
Year 2: $15,000
Year 3: $18,000

Average: ($12,000 + $15,000 + $18,000) ÷ 3 = $15,000
```

### Step 3: Calculate NPS

Subtract collection fees:
```
Average Gross: $15,000
Admin Fee (15%): -$2,250
NPS: $12,750
```

### Step 4: Apply Multiplier

Based on your catalog characteristics:
- New catalog, growing: 10x
- **Estimated Value: $127,500**

### Step 5: Adjust for Specific Factors

**Add value if:**
- You have songs trending on TikTok/viral
- Major artist is about to release your song
- Recent sync placement in big show

**Reduce value if:**
- Earnings are declining
- Your biggest hit is 10+ years old and fading
- Catalog is concentrated in one or two songs

## Reversion Rights and Their Impact on Value

### What Are Reversion Rights?

Reversion rights allow you to **terminate a publishing deal and get your rights back** after a certain period — typically 15-35 years after the grant of rights.

### How Reversion Affects Valuation

| Reversion Term | Impact on Value |
|----------------|-----------------|
| No reversion (life of copyright) | Higher immediate value, but you're locked in |
| 15 years | Standard — balances value and flexibility |
| 10 years | Lower value for buyer, better for you |
| 25+ years | Minimal impact on value |

### US Copyright Law (Section 203)

US law allows authors to terminate grants of rights:
- **Window:** 35-40 years after the grant
- **Notice:** Must be given 2-10 years before termination
- **Applies to:** Grants made on or after January 1, 1978

**Example:** If you signed a publishing deal in 2010, you could terminate it between 2045-2050.

### Negotiating Reversion

**What to ask for:**
- 15-year term with reversion
- Automatic reversion if advance isn't recouped in 5 years
- Reversion of unexploited songs after 10 years

**What publishers want:**
- Life of copyright
- Or 25+ year terms

**Compromise:** Life of copyright with reversion at 15 years post-term.

## Foreign Sub-Publishing: International Value

### What Is Foreign Sub-Publishing?

Foreign sub-publishing is when a publisher in another country collects your royalties locally. Most publishers use a network of sub-publishers worldwide.

### How It Works

```
Your Song → US Publisher → UK Sub-Publisher → UK PRO → Collection
                              ↓
                         UK Radio Play Money
                              ↓
                    Sub-Publisher Takes 10-25%
                              ↓
                    Remainder to US Publisher
```

### Territory Breakdown

| Territory | Typical Sub-Pub Fee | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| UK | 15-20% | Strong collection |
| Germany | 15% | GEMA is efficient |
| France | 15-20% | SACEM system |
| Japan | 15-25% | JASRAC |
| Australia | 10-15% | APRA |
| Brazil | 20-25% | Less efficient |
| Rest of World | 15-25% | Varies widely |

### Direct Registration vs. Sub-Publishing

**Direct Registration:**
- You register with foreign PROs directly
- No sub-publisher fees
- Complex — requires local bank accounts, tax IDs
- Only practical for major territories

**Sub-Publishing:**
- Your publisher handles everything
- Fees taken, but hassle-free
- Better for smaller catalogs

### When to Care About International

**You should prioritize international if:**
- Your music is streaming significantly overseas
- You have fans in Europe, Asia, or Latin America
- Your genre travels well (pop, EDM, hip-hop)
- You tour internationally

**You can deprioritize if:**
- Your audience is primarily US-based
- You're just starting out
- Your catalog is small

## Using Catalog Value in Negotiations

### Publishing Deal Negotiations

**If your catalog is worth $500K:**
- Don't accept an advance under $100K (20% of value)
- Negotiate for reversion at 15 years
- Keep your writer's share (non-negotiable)
- Consider keeping some songs out of the deal

### Catalog Sale Negotiations

**If selling:**
- Get multiple offers (Royalty Exchange, private buyers)
- Understand the tax implications (capital gains vs. ordinary income)
- Consider selling a portion (50% of catalog) instead of all
- Hire a specialized music attorney

### Securing Advances

**Using catalog as collateral:**
- Some companies (Paperchain, etc.) offer advances against royalties
- Typically 50-80% of expected annual earnings
- Fees are 5-15%
- Less expensive than selling, but still costly

## Tax Considerations

### Selling Your Catalog

**Capital Gains Treatment (US):**
- Catalog sales may qualify for capital gains (20% rate) vs. ordinary income (up to 37%)
- Must be a true sale, not a license
- Structure matters — consult a tax attorney

**Section 1221/1231:**
- Self-created works may have different treatment
- Professional songwriters vs. hobbyists treated differently

### Ongoing Royalties

- Royalties are ordinary income
- Deductible expenses: PRO dues, legal fees, demo costs, studio time
- Home office deduction if applicable

## When to Sell vs. Keep

### Reasons to Sell

1. **Immediate capital need** — Fund a business, buy a home, medical expenses
2. **Diversification** — Convert illiquid asset to diversified investments
3. **Declining earnings** — Get out before value drops further
4. **Estate planning** — Simplify inheritance
5. **Tax optimization** — Current year has offsetting losses

### Reasons to Keep

1. **Income stream** — Royalties provide ongoing cash flow
2. **Appreciation potential** — Catalogs can increase in value
3. **Leverage** — Use as collateral instead of selling
4. **Legacy** — Keep control of your work
5. **Tax deferral** — Pay tax as you earn, not all at once

### The Hybrid Approach

Sell a portion (25-50%) to:
- Get some immediate capital
- Keep some ongoing income
- Retain some upside potential
- Test the market before full sale

## Final Thoughts

Your catalog is likely worth more than you think — and understanding that value gives you power in negotiations. Whether you sell, keep, or leverage your catalog, make the decision with full information.

**Remember:**
- Catalogs are valued on multiples of NPS
- Multipliers range from 8x-18x (higher for exceptional catalogs)
- Reversion rights are crucial — negotiate them
- International collection adds significant value
- Get professional help for major transactions
