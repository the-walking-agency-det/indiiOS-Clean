# Royalty Sources & Streams

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-03-05  
**For:** indiiOS Platform

## Overview

Independent musicians leave money on the table by not understanding the royalty ecosystem. This document maps all revenue streams, registration requirements, and critical timing rules.

---

## 1. Performance Royalties (PROs)

**What:** Royalties when your music is performed publicly — radio, TV, streaming, live venues, restaurants, retail.

**Organizations:**
| PRO | Territory | Notes |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| **BMI** | US | Free for songwriters, $150-250 for publishers |
| **ASCAP** | US | $50 songwriter, $50 publisher |
| **SESAC** | US | Invite-only, higher payouts, more selective |
| **SOCAN** | Canada | BMI/ASCAP reciprocal agreements |
| **PRS** | UK | Similar reciprocal network |

**Critical Rule:**
> ⚠️ **Register BEFORE your music generates royalties.** PROs do NOT retroactively pay. If your song blows up on Spotify and you're not registered, that money is gone forever.

**Registration Requirements:**
- Legal name, address, SSN/Tax ID
- Stage name (if applicable)
- Banking info for direct deposit
- IPI/CAE number (assigned upon registration)

**Payout Schedule:** Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec)
- Payments lag 6-9 months behind actual usage
- Radio plays today = payment in 6-9 months

**Songwriter vs Publisher Share:**
- Standard split: 50% songwriter, 50% publisher
- If you don't have a publisher, you can claim both halves
- Indie artists should register as both

## 2. Digital Performance Royalties (SoundExchange)

**What:** Royalties for digital radio plays (Pandora, SiriusXM, iHeartRadio, webcasts) — NOT on-demand streaming like Spotify.

**Split Structure:**
| Rights Holder | Percentage |
|---------------|------------|
| Sound Recording Owner (label/artist) | 50% |
| Featured Artist | 45% |
| AFM/SAG-AFTRA (session musicians) | 5% |

**Critical Rule:**
> ⚠️ SoundExchange collects for the PERFORMER, not the songwriter. This is separate from PROs. You need BOTH.

**Registration:** Free at soundexchange.com
- Requires ISRC codes for each recording
- Must register each track individually
- Non-featured artists (session musicians) can claim through AFM/SAG-AFTRA

**Payout Schedule:** Monthly after threshold ($25-$100 depending on payment method)

---

## 3. Mechanical Royalties (MLC)

**What:** Royalties for reproduction/distribution of compositions — streaming, downloads, physical sales.

**The MLC (Mechanical Licensing Collective):**
- Created by Music Modernization Act 2018
- Collects from Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon, etc.
- Only handles mechanicals for STREAMING in the US

**Critical Rule:**
> ⚠️ Streaming services pay the MLC, who then pays songwriters/publishers. If you're not registered with MLC, you won't get paid for streams.

**Registration:** Free at themlc.com
- Requires IPI/CAE number (from PRO registration)
- Must register each work
- Self-published artists claim both songwriter AND publisher shares

**Payout Schedule:** Monthly

**Distinguish:**
- **Performance royalty** = the SONG (PROs handle this)
- **Mechanical royalty** = the REPRODUCTION (MLC handles this for streaming)
- A single Spotify stream generates BOTH

## 4. Distributor Royalties

**What:** Revenue from actual sales/streams — Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon, YouTube, TikTok, etc.

**Major Distributors:**
| Distributor | Fee Structure | Key Features |
|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| **DistroKid** | $22.99/year unlimited | Fast, keeps 0% royalties |
| **TuneCore** | $14.99/single, $29.99/album + renewal | Was 100% royalty, now keeps 20% |
| **CD Baby** | $9.95/single, $29/album | One-time fee, keeps 9% |
| **Amuse** | Free tier available | Slower, keeps 0% on free tier |
| **UnitedMasters** | Free or $59.99/year | Selectable distribution tiers |

**Critical Rules:**

### Trailing Royalties
When you switch distributors, your OLD distributor still holds royalties earned while you were with them. You must:
1. Notify old distributor of move
2. Keep account active (don't delete)
3. Download all statements before closing
4. Some distributors hold funds for 6-12 months after last release

### "Sell Before You Stream" Strategy
> ⚠️ **Register with your PRO and complete all registrations BEFORE releasing music.** If you release first and register later, you lose the early streaming royalties permanently.

### Distributor ≠ PRO
Distributors pay you for the MASTER RECORDING (the actual audio file). PROs pay you for the COMPOSITION (the song itself). These are separate revenue streams.

## 5. Sync Licensing

**What:** Licensing your music for TV, film, commercials, video games, trailers.

**How It Works:**
- Negotiated directly or through sync agent/library
- Two licenses required:
  - **Master use license** (for the recording) — controlled by artist/label
  - **Sync license** (for the composition) — controlled by songwriter/publisher
- Fees vary wildly: $500 for indie podcast to $500K+ for major commercial

**Registration:**
- No central registry
- Work with music supervisors directly
- Use platforms like Songtradr, Musicbed, Artlist, Epidemic Sound
- Register with Harry Fox Agency for mechanical sync licensing

---

## 6. Neighboring Rights

**What:** Performance royalties for sound recordings outside the US.

**Territories:** UK, EU, Canada, Australia, etc. (NOT US)

**Collection:**
- PPL (UK)
- GVL (Germany)
- SENA (Netherlands)
- Various others by country

**Note:** US has no neighboring rights law. This is money left on the table for US artists with international play.

---

## 7. YouTube Content ID

**What:** Monetize when others use your music in their videos.

**How:**
- Distributor submits your music to YouTube's Content ID system
- YouTube scans uploads for matches
- You earn ad revenue from videos using your music

**Important:**
- Don't Content ID your own channel (you'll claim against yourself)
- DistroKid/others offer "YouTube Monetization" as add-on
- Revenue split: YouTube 45%, rights holder 55%

## Summary: What You Need to Register For

| Stream | Registration | Cost | When |
|--------|--------------|------|------|
| Radio/TV/Streaming (songwriter) | BMI/ASCAP/SESAC | Free-$250 | BEFORE release |
| Digital radio (performer) | SoundExchange | Free | ASAP |
| Streaming mechanicals | MLC | Free | After PRO (need IPI) |
| Physical/digital sales | Distributor | Varies | Anytime |
| TV/Film/Commercials | Direct/agent | Varies | As opportunities arise |
| International performance | Neighboring rights orgs | Varies | If played internationally |
| YouTube claims | Via distributor | Varies | Recommended |

---

## Critical Timing Rules

1. **PRO First** — Register with BMI/ASCAP before releasing anything
2. **MLC Second** — Register with MLC after you have IPI number from PRO
3. **SoundExchange Third** — Register anytime, but sooner = more money
4. **Distributor Last** — You can switch distributors, but you can't recover lost PRO/MLC royalties

**The Golden Rule:**
> Register everywhere BEFORE the money starts flowing. Retroactive registration = lost money.
