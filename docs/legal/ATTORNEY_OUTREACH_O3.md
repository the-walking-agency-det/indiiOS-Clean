# O3 — Outreach Message Drafts
### For: Platform Legal Partner pitch · March 2026
### Confidential — do not commit identifying info until deal is signed

---

## Which channel to use

| Channel | When to use it |
|---|---|
| **Instagram / Twitter DM** | If you already follow each other or she's active in DMs. Shorter message, low-friction. The goal is one reply, not a close. |
| **Email** | If you have her firm's email. Higher signal, easier to share the deck as an attachment. More professional for a business proposal. |
| **YouTube comment → DM** | Cold fallback only. Comment publicly on a recent relevant video, then DM referencing it. Lowest response rate. Don't start here. |

**Recommendation:** Email first. If you have a direct address, use it. If
not, her law firm's general contact works — the subject line below will
route it to her.

---

## Sending checklist

Before you hit send:

- [ ] Confirm you have her direct email / DM handle (not the firm's
      generic contact form if you can avoid it)
- [ ] Export `ATTORNEY_PARTNERSHIP_DECK.md` to PDF — cleaner to attach
      than a markdown file
- [ ] Have a calendar link ready (Calendly, Cal.com, or Google Calendar
      appointment slots) — if she says yes, the next step should be
      one click
- [ ] Know which deal structure you'd open with (Hybrid / Option C is
      lowest friction — acknowledge the platform is early, no upfront
      cost, rev-share after traction)
- [ ] Do NOT send the attorney checklist or any legal draft details in
      the first message — the pitch is the partnership opportunity only

---

## After she replies (if yes)

1. Send the deck (already done — `ATTORNEY_PARTNERSHIP_DECK.md`)
2. Book the call
3. On the call: demo the app live — show the "Find Counsel" screen
   with the placeholder, walk her through a distribution flow, show
   the contract analysis tool. This is a product demo as much as a
   business pitch.
4. After the call: send a follow-up with the specific deal terms you're
   proposing (Option C — hybrid) and a timeline
5. O4: Agree on structure → O5: Get the partnership agreement reviewed
   → O6: Integrate her real details → O7: Co-announce

---

## After she replies (if not right now)

No is not always no — it's often "not yet" or "I don't understand it yet."

- If she declines: thank her, ask if she'd like to be kept in the loop
  at launch. Keep her on the list.
- If she doesn't reply: one follow-up after 7 days is appropriate.
  Subject: `Re: Platform partnership — following up`. Two lines max.
  After that, move on.
- If the platform has grown when you reconnect: that's the re-open.
  "We launched, here's the traction, the slot is still available."
