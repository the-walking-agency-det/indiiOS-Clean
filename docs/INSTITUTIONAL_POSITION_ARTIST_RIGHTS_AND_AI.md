# indiiOS Institutional Position
## Artist Rights, AI Ethics, and Platform Integrity

**Status:** Foundational policy draft  
**Scope:** Product design, legal architecture, partner contracts, AI tooling, and distribution operations.

---

## Purpose

This document defines the institutional stance of indiiOS as we scale. It is a foundational statement of what we build, what we refuse to build, and how we protect creators while deploying advanced AI systems.

We believe long-term platform value comes from:
- artist trust,
- rights clarity,
- transparent economics,
- and technically enforceable ethics.

---

## Institutional Commitments

### 1) Explicit Consent for Transformative AI
No voice, likeness, or composition-level transformation without explicit authorization.

**Operational requirements:**
- Permission must be granular (by right type and use case).
- Consent records must be auditable and revocable.
- Default state is opt-out unless artist opts in clearly.

### 2) No Legacy-Contract Exploitation
We do not reinterpret outdated contract language to claim rights for new AI behaviors.

**Operational requirements:**
- New capabilities require new explicit terms.
- Rights scope must be human-readable and versioned.
- Silent policy updates cannot expand exploitation rights.

### 3) Respect Finished Art as Finished Art
Music is not treated as generic "raw material" for derivative extraction pipelines.

**Operational requirements:**
- Creator intent is preserved in product defaults.
- Transformative features are permission-gated.
- Attribution and lineage are maintained for derivative outputs.

### 4) No Dark-Pattern Rights Capture
We reject rights acquisition through confusing distributor toggles, buried disclaimers, or deceptive UX.

**Operational requirements:**
- Consent UX must be plain-language and unambiguous.
- High-impact rights choices require explicit confirmation steps.
- Dashboard design should reduce accidental rights surrender.

### 5) Anti-Lock-In Creator Economics
We reject business models that force creators into closed AI ecosystems to access their own audience.

**Operational requirements:**
- Interoperable exports and standards-first data pathways.
- Transparent entitlement and licensing rules.
- Creator control over where and how content is monetized.

### 6) Zero-Tolerance Fraud Posture
We reject fake-stream economies and synthetic abuse patterns.

**Operational requirements:**
- Continuous anti-bot and fraud detection pipelines.
- Financial controls against laundering behaviors.
- Automated anomaly reviews with enforcement and appeals.

---

## Governance + Enforcement Model

To make this institutional position real, each principle must map to code-level controls:

1. **Policy-as-code gates:** enforce consent checks before AI transformations.
2. **Event audit trails:** immutable logs for rights decisions and model actions.
3. **Contract lineage system:** rights provenance tracked from ingestion to payout.
4. **Release controls:** no launch of high-risk features without legal + safety signoff.
5. **Quarterly audits:** review violations, false positives, and remediation timelines.

---

## Product Implications

- Feature velocity does not override rights clarity.
- "Possible with AI" is not equal to "permitted by policy."
- Growth KPIs must be paired with trust and integrity KPIs.

---

## Closing Position

indiiOS is building a creator-first system where technical excellence and ethical integrity are inseparable.

Our institutional stance is clear:
- powerful AI,
- strict consent,
- transparent rights,
- and fraud-resistant economics.
