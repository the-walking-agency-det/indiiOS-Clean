# BMI vs ASCAP vs SESAC: Which is Right for You?

**For:** indiiOS Artists  
**Reading Time:** 4 minutes  
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## The Short Answer

| If you... | Choose |
|-----------|--------|
| Want free registration | **BMI** |
| Want member control/voting rights | **ASCAP** |
| Are established with significant catalog | **SESAC** |
| Make hip-hop, R&B, or pop | **BMI** |
| Make pop, rock, or country | **ASCAP** |
| Want monthly payouts | **SESAC** |

---

## Detailed Comparison

### Cost

**BMI:**
- Songwriter: **Free**
- Publisher: $150-250 one-time
- **Best for:** Artists on a budget

**ASCAP:**
- Songwriter: **$50/year**
- Publisher: $50/year
- **Best for:** Artists who want member benefits

**SESAC:**
- Songwriter: **Invite-only**
- Publisher: Invite-only
- **Best for:** Established artists

**Winner:** BMI (free)

---

### Payout Frequency

**BMI:** Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec)

**ASCAP:** Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec)

**SESAC:** Monthly

**Winner:** SESAC (monthly)

---

### Payout Amounts

This is the big question everyone asks, and the answer is: **it depends.**

**Factors that affect your payout:**
- Where your music is played (radio, TV, streaming, live)
- How often it's played
- The specific license agreements your PRO has
- Your genre and market

**General observations:**
- **BMI:** Strong in urban/hip-hop, large catalog
- **ASCAP:** Strong in pop/rock, songwriter-focused
- **SESAC:** Selective, higher per-play rates for members

**Winner:** Tie (depends on your genre and usage)

---

### Genre Strengths

**BMI:**
- Hip-hop and R&B
- Pop
- Urban music
- Electronic/EDM

**ASCAP:**
- Pop
- Rock
- Country
- Singer-songwriter

**SESAC:**
- All genres (selective membership)
- Strong in Christian/gospel
- Jazz and classical

**Winner:** Depends on your genre

---

### Membership Control

**BMI:**
- For-profit company
- No member voting
- Professional management

**ASCAP:**
- Member-owned nonprofit
- Members vote on board
- Songwriter-focused governance

**SESAC:**
- For-profit company
- Invitation-only
- Personalized service

**Winner:** ASCAP (if member control matters to you)

---

### International Representation

All three PROs have reciprocal agreements with international PROs:
- If your music plays overseas, your US PRO collects and pays you
- If you're registered with any major US PRO, you're covered globally

**Winner:** Tie (all have strong international networks)

## Real-World Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Independent Artist

**Profile:** Just starting, releasing first single, limited budget

**Recommendation:** **BMI**

**Why:** Free registration means no upfront cost. You can always switch later if needed. The quarterly payouts are standard, and BMI's large catalog means broad representation.

---

### Scenario 2: Established Songwriter

**Profile:** Multiple releases, co-writing regularly, serious about publishing

**Recommendation:** **ASCAP**

**Why:** The $50/year is negligible compared to potential royalties. Member ownership means you have a voice in the organization. Strong songwriter community and resources.

---

### Scenario 3: Artist with Radio Play

**Profile:** Getting significant radio airplay, need faster payouts

**Recommendation:** **SESAC** (if you can get invited)

**Why:** Monthly payouts mean cash flow 3x faster than quarterly. Higher per-play rates can add up with significant airplay. Personalized service for complex situations.

---

### Scenario 4: Hip-Hop Producer

**Profile:** Making beats, collaborating with vocalists, releasing frequently

**Recommendation:** **BMI**

**Why:** BMI has the strongest representation in hip-hop and urban music. Free registration means you can sign up multiple collaborators without cost barriers.

---

## Can You Switch?

**Yes, but it's complicated:**

1. **Waiting period:** Usually 1-2 years after joining
2. **Written request:** Must submit formal termination letter
3. **Work transfer:** Re-register all your songs with new PRO
4. **Royalty gap:** Potential for missed payments during transition

**Advice:** Choose carefully upfront. Switching is possible but messy.

---

## What About Co-Writers?

**Each songwriter chooses their own PRO.**

Example:
- You register with BMI
- Your co-writer registers with ASCAP
- The song generates $100 in royalties
- BMI pays you your share
- ASCAP pays your co-writer their share
- The PROs handle the split between them

**You don't need to match your co-writer's PRO.**

---

## The Bottom Line

**For most independent artists starting out:**

→ **Choose BMI**

**Reasons:**
- Free registration removes financial barrier
- Strong in popular genres (hip-hop, pop, R&B)
- Large catalog = broad representation
- Easy online process

**When to consider ASCAP:**
- You want member ownership/voting
- $50/year is not a concern
- You prefer their songwriter community

**When to consider SESAC:**
- You're established with significant catalog
- You receive an invitation
- Monthly payouts are critical for cash flow

```
Are you just starting out?
├── Yes → BMI (free)
└── No → Are you established with radio play?
    ├── Yes → SESAC (if invited)
    └── No → Do you want member control?
        ├── Yes → ASCAP

```

## Still Unsure?

**Talk to other artists in your genre.** What PRO are they with? Are they happy?

**Check with your collaborators.** If everyone you work with is on BMI, there might be community benefits.

**Remember:** The most important thing is registering with **some** PRO. The difference between BMI and ASCAP is smaller than the difference between being registered and not being registered.

---

**Start with BMI.** It's free, it's easy, and you can always switch later if your needs change.
