# How to Register with a PRO

**For:** indiiOS Artists  
**Reading Time:** 5 minutes  
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## What is a PRO?

A Performance Rights Organization (PRO) collects royalties when your music is performed publicly — radio, TV, streaming, live venues, restaurants, retail stores.

**The three major PROs in the US:**
- **BMI** (Broadcast Music, Inc.)
- **ASCAP** (American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers)
- **SESAC** (Society of European Stage Authors and Composers)

---

## Which PRO Should You Choose?

### BMI

**Best for:** Most independent artists

**Pros:**
- Free for songwriters
- Largest catalog, broad representation
- Strong in hip-hop, R&B, pop
- Easy online registration

**Cons:**
- Publisher registration costs $150-250
- Quarterly payouts only

**Cost:** Free (songwriter) / $150-250 (publisher)

**Signup:** bmi.com/join

---

### ASCAP

**Best for:** Songwriters who want more control

**Pros:**
- Songwriter-focused organization
- Member-owned (you have a vote)
- Strong in pop, rock, country
- Good international representation

**Cons:**
- $50/year membership fee
- Must renew annually

**Cost:** $50/year (songwriter) / $50/year (publisher)

**Signup:** ascap.com/join

---

### SESAC

**Best for:** Established artists with significant catalog

**Pros:**
- Invite-only (more selective)
- Monthly payouts (faster)
- Higher per-play rates
- Personalized service

**Cons:**
- Must be invited or apply
- Not open to everyone
- Smaller catalog

**Cost:** Invitation only

**Signup:** sesac.com (request invitation)

---

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | BMI | ASCAP | SESAC |
|---------|-----|-------|-------|
| **Cost (songwriter)** | Free | $50/year | Invite-only |
| **Cost (publisher)** | $150-250 | $50/year | Invite-only |
| **Payout frequency** | Quarterly | Quarterly | Monthly |
| **Best genres** | Hip-hop, R&B, Pop | Pop, Rock, Country | All |
| **Open to all** | Yes | Yes | No |
| **Member-owned** | No | Yes | No |

## Registration Process

### Step 1: Choose Your PRO

Consider:
- **Cost:** BMI is free, ASCAP is $50/year
- **Genre:** Each PRO has strengths in different genres
- **Payout timing:** SESAC pays monthly, others quarterly
- **Your network:** What do your collaborators use?

**You can only be with one PRO at a time.** If you switch later, there's a waiting period and paperwork.

---

### Step 2: Gather Required Information

You'll need:
- **Legal name** (as it appears on your tax documents)
- **Mailing address**
- **Social Security Number or Tax ID** (EIN)
- **Date of birth**
- **Stage name** (if different from legal name)
- **Banking information** (for direct deposit)

**For publisher registration (optional but recommended):**
- Publishing company name (can be your stage name + "Music" or "Publishing")
- Same personal information as above

---

### Step 3: Complete Online Application

**BMI:**
1. Go to bmi.com/join
2. Select "Songwriter" or "Songwriter & Publisher"
3. Create account with email/password
4. Fill out personal information
5. Add publishing company (if applicable)
6. Submit application

**ASCAP:**
1. Go to ascap.com/join
2. Select "Writer" or "Writer & Publisher"
3. Pay membership fee ($50)
4. Fill out personal information
5. Add publishing company (if applicable)
6. Submit application

**SESAC:**
1. Go to sesac.com
2. Request invitation or application
3. Wait for approval (if invited, proceed)
4. Complete application process

---

### Step 4: Wait for IPI Number

After submitting, you'll receive:
- **Confirmation email** — Usually within 24 hours
- **IPI/CAE number** — Your unique identifier (5-9 business days)

**What is an IPI number?**
- International standard identifier for songwriters
- Required to register with the MLC
- Used to track your royalties worldwide

**Example:** 123456789

---

### Step 5: Set Up Direct Deposit

Once approved:
1. Log into your PRO account
2. Navigate to "Payment Information" or "Banking"
3. Add your bank account for direct deposit
4. Set payment threshold (optional — minimum amount before they pay)

**Without direct deposit:** Checks are mailed (slower, potential for loss)

## Songwriter vs Publisher Registration

### Songwriter (Writer) Share: 50%
This is you, the creator of the music and lyrics.

### Publisher Share: 50%
This is the entity that "publishes" your work. If you don't have a publisher, you can claim this share yourself.

**As an independent artist, you should register as BOTH:**
- Songwriter: Collects your 50% writer share
- Publisher: Collects your 50% publisher share
- **Total: 100% of performance royalties**

**If you only register as a songwriter:** You only get 50%. The other 50% sits unclaimed.

## After Registration

### Register Your Works

Once you have your IPI number, register each song:
1. Log into your PRO account
2. Go to "Register Works" or "Catalog"
3. Enter song title, co-writers, splits
4. Add ISWC if you have it (optional)

**Do this BEFORE releasing music.**

### Update indiiOS

In your indiiOS profile:
1. Go to Settings → Royalties
2. Select your PRO
3. Enter your IPI number
4. Mark registration as complete

This enables the platform to track your status and alert you to missing registrations.

## Common Issues

### "My name is already taken"
If your stage name conflicts with another artist, you may need to use a variation or your legal name.

### "I don't have a Tax ID"
Use your Social Security Number. You can get an EIN later if you form an LLC.

### "I co-wrote a song, they're with a different PRO"
Each songwriter registers with their own PRO. The PROs handle the split between them.

### "I want to switch PROs"
You can, but there's a waiting period (usually 1-2 years) and paperwork. Choose carefully upfront.
