# Understanding Music Royalties: The Complete Guide

**For:** indiiOS Artists  
**Reading Time:** 8 minutes  
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## The Big Picture

When your music gets played, money is generated. But that money doesn't automatically find its way to you. It goes into a complex system of organizations, each handling a different type of royalty. Your job is to register with the right organizations so they know where to send your money.

**The Golden Rule:** Register **before** your music generates royalties. These organizations do not pay retroactively. If you're not registered when the play happens, that money is gone forever.

---

## The Four Main Royalty Streams

### 1. Performance Royalties (PROs)

**What it is:** Money when your music is performed publicly — radio, TV, streaming, live venues, restaurants, retail stores.

**Who collects it:** Performance Rights Organizations (PROs)
- **BMI** — Free for songwriters, most popular
- **ASCAP** — $50/year, songwriter-focused
- **SESAC** — Invite-only, higher payouts

**Who gets paid:** Songwriters and publishers

**How it works:**
1. Radio station plays your song
2. Station pays license fees to PROs
3. PRO tracks the play
4. PRO pays you (quarterly, 6-9 months later)

**Critical:** You must register **before** the play happens. PROs do not backpay.

---

### 2. Digital Performance Royalties (SoundExchange)

**What it is:** Money when your music plays on digital radio — Pandora, SiriusXM, iHeartRadio, webcasts.

**Who collects it:** SoundExchange

**Who gets paid:** Performers (not songwriters)

**The Split:**
- 50% — Sound recording owner (you or your label)
- 45% — Featured artist (you)
- 5% — Session musicians (via union)

**Why it matters:** This is **separate** from PROs. Even if you're registered with BMI, you're not getting SoundExchange money unless you register separately.

---

### 3. Mechanical Royalties (MLC)

**What it is:** Money for the reproduction of your composition — streaming, downloads, physical sales.

**Who collects it:** The Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC)

**Who gets paid:** Songwriters and publishers

**How it's different:**
- **PROs** = the performance of the song
- **MLC** = the reproduction/copying of the song
- A single Spotify stream generates **both**

**Example:**
- You write a song and release it on Spotify
- PRO pays you for the "performance" (someone listened)
- MLC pays you for the "mechanical" (a copy was made to buffer/stream)

**Requirement:** You need an IPI number from your PRO registration first.

---

### 4. Distributor Revenue

**What it is:** Your share of actual sales and streams — Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon, YouTube, TikTok.

**Who collects it:** Your distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby, etc.)

**Who gets paid:** You (the artist/label)

**Important distinction:**
- **Distributor** pays for the master recording (the actual audio file)
- **PRO/MLC** pay for the composition (the song itself)
- These are separate revenue streams

---

## The Registration Order

You can't do everything at once. Here's the correct order:

### Step 1: PRO (Day 1)
Register with BMI, ASCAP, or SESAC. This is your foundation.

**Why first:** You need the IPI number from your PRO to register with the MLC.

### Step 2: SoundExchange (Day 2-7)
Register while waiting for your PRO application to process.

**Why now:** No dependencies. Can be done in parallel.

### Step 3: MLC (Day 14-21)
Register after you receive your IPI number from your PRO.

**Why wait:** The MLC requires your IPI number.

### Step 4: Distributor (Anytime)
Choose and set up your distributor.

**Why anytime:** Distributors don't depend on PRO/MLC registration.

## Common Mistakes

### "I'll register after my song blows up"
**Wrong.** PROs, SoundExchange, and the MLC do not pay retroactively. If your song gets 1 million streams before you're registered, you get $0 for those streams.

### "My distributor handles all my royalties"
**Wrong.** Distributors only pay for the master recording. They don't collect PRO, SoundExchange, or MLC royalties. You must register separately.

### "I'm registered with BMI, so I'm covered"
**Partially wrong.** BMI only covers performance royalties. You still need SoundExchange (digital performance) and MLC (mechanicals) to collect everything.

### "I don't need to register until I make money"
**Wrong.** By the time you "make money," you've already lost the royalties from your first plays. Registration must happen **before** release.

## The "Sell Before You Stream" Strategy

**The Problem:** Once your music is on streaming services, the clock starts ticking. If you're not registered with your PRO, those plays generate royalties you'll never see.

**The Solution:** 
1. Complete all registrations **before** releasing
2. Consider a direct-to-fan release first (Bandcamp, your website)
3. Build buzz and sales
4. Then release to streaming platforms

**Why it works:**
- You control the timeline
- You keep more money (no streaming platform cut)
- You build a fanbase that will stream when you do release
- All your registrations are active when streaming starts

## How Much Money Are We Talking?

**Performance Royalties (PRO):**
- Radio play: $50-500 per play (varies by station size)
- Streaming: ~$0.0007 per stream (small, but adds up)
- Live venues: Varies by venue size and reporting

**Digital Performance (SoundExchange):**
- Pandora: ~$0.0014 per play
- SiriusXM: ~$0.50-2.00 per play
- Web radio: Varies widely

**Mechanical Royalties (MLC):**
- Streaming: ~$0.0005 per stream
- Physical/download: ~$0.09 per unit

**Distributor Revenue:**
- Spotify: ~$0.003-0.005 per stream
- Apple Music: ~$0.01 per stream
- YouTube: ~$0.0005-0.002 per view

**Example:** 100,000 Spotify streams
- Distributor: ~$300-500
- PRO (performance): ~$70
- MLC (mechanical): ~$50
- **Total: ~$420-620**

Without PRO and MLC registration, you only get the $300-500 from your distributor. You're leaving 30-40% on the table.

## The Quarterly Cycle

Most royalties pay quarterly, but with significant lag:

| Quarter | Period | PRO Payment | MLC Payment |
|---------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Q1 | Jan-Mar | May | Apr |
| Q2 | Apr-Jun | Aug | Jul |
| Q3 | Jul-Sep | Nov | Oct |
| Q4 | Oct-Dec | Feb | Jan |

**Lag time:** 3-6 months from play to payment.

**What this means:** If you release in January, your first PRO payment for those plays comes in May. Your first MLC payment comes in April.
