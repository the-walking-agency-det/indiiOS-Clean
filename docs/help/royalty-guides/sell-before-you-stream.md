# Sell Before You Stream: The Release Strategy That Protects Your Royalties

**For:** indiiOS Artists  
**Reading Time:** 6 minutes  
**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## The Problem

When you release music directly to Spotify, Apple Music, and other streaming platforms, the clock starts ticking immediately. Every stream generates royalties — but only if you're registered with the right organizations.

**If you're not registered:**
- Your PRO can't pay you (no IPI number)
- The MLC can't pay you (no IPI number)
- SoundExchange can't pay you (not registered)
- **That money is gone forever**

---

## The Solution: Sell Before You Stream

**The strategy:**
1. Complete all registrations **first**
2. Release directly to fans (Bandcamp, your website, email list)
3. Build buzz and make direct sales
4. **Then** release to streaming platforms

**Why it works:**
- You control the timeline
- You keep more money (no platform cut on direct sales)
- All your registrations are active when streaming starts
- You build a fanbase that will stream when you release

---

## The Timeline

### Phase 1: Registration (Weeks 1-3)

**Week 1:**
- Register with BMI, ASCAP, or SESAC
- Register with SoundExchange

**Week 2-3:**
- Wait for IPI number from PRO
- Register with MLC (requires IPI)
- Set up distributor account

**Status check:** All registrations complete? Move to Phase 2.

---

### Phase 2: Direct-to-Fan Release (Weeks 4-6)

**Platforms:**
- **Bandcamp** — Best for artists, keeps 85-90%
- **Your website** — Full control, highest margin
- **Email list** — Direct to your biggest fans
- **Social media** — Tease, build anticipation

**What to release:**
- Full album or EP
- Exclusive content (behind-the-scenes, liner notes)
- Limited editions (signed copies, merch bundles)
- Early access (fans get it before streaming)

**Goals:**
- Make direct sales (you keep 85-100% vs 70% from streaming)
- Build email list
- Create buzz for streaming release
- Test marketing messages

---

### Phase 3: Streaming Release (Week 7+)

**Now you're ready:**
- All registrations active
- IPI number in hand
- PRO, MLC, SoundExchange all set
- Fanbase primed and ready

**Release to:**
- Spotify
- Apple Music
- Amazon Music
- YouTube Music
- All other platforms

**Result:** Every stream generates the maximum possible royalty, and you're set up to collect it all.

## The Numbers

### Direct Sales (Bandcamp)

| Revenue | You Keep |
|---------|----------|
| $10 album sale | $8.50-9.00 (85-90%) |
| $1 single sale | $0.85-0.90 (85-90%) |

### Streaming (Spotify)

| Revenue | You Keep |
|---------|----------|
| 1,000 streams (~$3-5) | $2.10-3.50 (70% after distributor) |

**Comparison:**
- 100 direct album sales = $850-900
- 100,000 streams = $300-500

**Direct sales are worth 170-300x more per fan.**

---

## Why This Matters for Royalties

### Scenario A: Stream First (Wrong)

**Week 1:** Release to Spotify
- 10,000 streams in first week
- Not registered with PRO yet
- **Lost royalties:** ~$7 (performance) + ~$5 (mechanical) = **$12 gone forever**

**Week 3:** Finally register with PRO
- Get IPI number
- Register with MLC

**Week 4+:** Streams continue
- Now you're collecting
- But you lost the first 10,000 streams permanently

---

### Scenario B: Sell Before You Stream (Right)

**Weeks 1-3:** Complete all registrations
- PRO: Active
- SoundExchange: Active
- MLC: Active

**Weeks 4-6:** Direct-to-fan release
- 100 album sales = $850
- Build email list of 200 fans
- Create buzz

**Week 7:** Release to streaming
- 10,000 streams in first week
- All registrations active
- **Royalties collected:** ~$7 (performance) + ~$5 (mechanical) + ~$35 (distributor) = **$47 collected**

**Plus:** You already made $850 from direct sales.

**Total:** $897 vs $35 (streaming only, no direct sales)

## The "Windowing" Strategy

**Windowing** = releasing to different platforms at different times

### Common Windowing Schedule

| Week | Platform | Strategy |
|------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Direct (Bandcamp, website) | Exclusive early access for fans |
| 2-3 | Direct + email | Continue direct sales push |
| 4 | Streaming | Wide release to all platforms |
| 5+ | All platforms | Full availability |

**Why it works:**
- Rewards your biggest fans with early access
- Maximizes direct sales (highest margin)
- Builds anticipation for streaming release
- Ensures all registrations are active

## Addressing Concerns

### "But I want my music everywhere immediately"

**The trade-off:** Immediate availability vs. maximum revenue and royalty protection.

**Consider:** A 3-4 week delay is negligible in the lifespan of a song. The lost royalties from rushing are permanent.

---

### "What if someone uploads my music to YouTube?"

**Register with Content ID first:**
- Most distributors offer YouTube Content ID
- This protects your music on YouTube regardless of release strategy
- Can be done before streaming release

---

### "My fans will be mad if it's not on Spotify"

**Communicate the strategy:**
- "Exclusive early access for supporters on Bandcamp"
- "Streaming release coming [date]"
- Frame it as a perk, not a restriction

**Your true fans will:**
- Buy direct to support you
- Stream later anyway
- Appreciate the transparency

---

### "What about pre-saves?"

**Pre-saves are fine, but:**
- They don't generate revenue
- They don't require registration
- You can still do direct-to-fan first

**Timeline:**
- Weeks 1-3: Registrations + direct release
- Week 4: Pre-save campaign starts
- Week 5: Streaming release
