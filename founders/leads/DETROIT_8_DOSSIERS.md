# Detroit 8: Strategic Dossiers
## The Route to Digital Sovereignty

### 1. Pax (Sam Fotias / Paxahau)
*   **Pain Points:** Massive administrative overhead for Movement Festival (metadata collection, contract signatures, payment splits for 100+ artists). High cost of "aggregator" middlemen for festival-owned labels.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Sovereign Stage**. indii becomes the official technical backbone for the festival. Artists manage their own "Vaults" and splits, and Pax gets a clean, unified data feed for the entire lineup.

### 2. Sam Valenti IV (Ghostly International)
*   **Pain Points:** Managing a multi-disciplinary roster (Art, Design, Electronic, Indie). Fragmented tools for managing masters vs. merch vs. digital art.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Ghost in the Machine**. indii as the "Aesthetic Command Center." Use the "Universe" branding. Show how the Sound Locker and Tapstitch integration bridges the gap between a "Track" and a "Design Drop."

### 3. Carl Craig (Planet E)
*   **Pain Points:** Maintaining legacy status in a "streaming-first" world. Protecting the archival masters of 30+ years of Detroit Techno.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Legacy Vault**. indii isn't for "disposable" music; it's for the architects. Focus on the military-grade encryption and the "Digital Homecoming" for his massive back-catalog.

### 4. Moodymann (Mahogani Music)
*   **Pain Points:** Intense desire for privacy and independence. Distrust of centralized cloud platforms. High manual labor for label administration.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Private Press 4.0**. indii is local-first. No cloud-first surveillance. The music stays in his building until he decides it moves. It’s the digital version of "cutting a dubplate."

### 5. Third Man Records (Jack White / Archive)
*   **Pain Points:** The friction between physical (Vinyl) and digital (Streaming). The need for high-fidelity archival security for unreleased masters.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **High-Fidelity Bridge**. indii's local spectral forensics ensures the digital version matches the quality of the physical press. The Vault as the "Digital Fort Knox" for the Third Man archives.

### 6. UR (Mad Mike Banks / Underground Resistance)
*   **Pain Points:** Corporate exploitation. The "Programmer" class taking the power. The "Digital Plantation" model of modern streaming.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Technical Weapon.** indii is the implementation of UR philosophy in code. "He who owns the code controls the universe." Zero middleman tax. Zero corporate surveillance.

### 7. Todd Johnson (Charivari / Sillygirlie)
*   **Pain Points:** Navigating the industry for a new artist (Sillygirlie) while scaling an independent festival (Charivari).
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Generation Gap Solution.** The "Autonomous Office" handles the boring legal/tax stuff for Sillygirlie, while providing a scalable infrastructure for Charivari's future lineup.

### 8. DTM (Detroit Techno Militia)
*   **Pain Points:** High technical requirements for label management. The need for speed from "Studio to Store." Coordinating a collective of individual labels.
*   **The "indii" Play:** Pitch the **Militia Grid.** indii as a decentralized network of labels. Focus on the **Velocity Engine**—getting the "Heat" from the mixer to Beatport in hours.
