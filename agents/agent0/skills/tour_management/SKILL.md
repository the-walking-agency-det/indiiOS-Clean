# Tour Management & Live Operations (Agent Zero Skill)

**Domain Expert:** Tour Manager / Booking Agent  
**Goal:** Optimize tour routing, ensure profitable margins, manage logistics, and interface with venues/promoters.

## 1. Routing & Logistics Strategy

- **Anchor Dates:** Always book anchor dates (festival plays or guaranteed high-grossing weekend markets) first. Fill in the "routing dates" (Tues-Thurs) along the path to cover overhead.
- **Drive Times:** Never route back-to-back shows with >8 hours of drive time without a dedicated driver. Account for load-in times (usually 2PM-4PM).
- **Day Sheets:** Every day on tour requires a Day Sheet detailing:
  - Hotel checkout, Lobby Call, Drive Time, Venue Address, Load-in, Soundcheck, Doors, Set Time, Load-out, Hotel.

## 2. Tour Budgeting & Deals

- **Guarantee vs. Door Deal vs. Versus Deal:**
  - **Guarantee:** Fixed fee regardless of attendance.
  - **Door Deal:** Percentage of ticket sales after expenses (Door splits usually 70/30 or 80/20 in favor of artist).
  - **Versus Deal:** The artist gets the Guarantee OR the Percentage, whichever is higher.
  - **Plus Deal:** Guarantee PLUS a percentage of the backend after promoter expenses.
- **Tour Overheads:** Account for vehicle rental, fuel (calculate MPG based on routing), hotels, per diems (standard $30-$50/day/person), crew pay, insurance, and staging/production costs. Always pad the budget by 10% for emergencies.

## 3. Advancing the Show

- **The Advance:** Reach out to the venue/promoter 2-4 weeks prior.
- **Tech Rider:** Input list, stage plot, power requirements, backline sharing.
- **Hospitality Rider:** Green room requirements, dietary restrictions, buyout vs. provided meals (Buyout is often preferred: $20-$30 per traveling member).
- **Settlement:** The process of getting paid at the end of the night. Always get the settlement sheet signed by the promoter and verify ticket counts vs. capacity.
