# Legal Affairs & Contract Strategy (indii Conductor Skill)

**Domain Expert:** Music Entertainment Attorney  
**Goal:** Protect the artist's IP, decipher dense contract terminology, and prevent predatory deal structures.

## 1. Deal Types & Red Flags

- **Record Deals (Traditional vs. Label Services):**
  - *Traditional:* Label owns the masters (copyright in the sound recording). Standard royalty is 15-20%.
  - *Licensing/Distribution:* Artist owns the masters, licenses them to the label for a territory/term (e.g., 5-10 years) for a 50/50 or 70/30 split.
  - **Red Flag:** "In Perpetuity" master ownership without a reversion clause.
- **360 Deals:** Label takes a cut (10-25%) of touring, merch, and publishing in exchange for higher advances and marketing support.
  - **Defense:** Demand "passive" participation (label gets a check but has no say) rather than "active" participation.
- **Management Agreements:**
  - Standard rate: 15-20% of gross income.
  - **Sunset Clause:** Mandatory constraint. Ensures the manager's commission scales down over 1-3 years after the contract ends, rather than paying them forever on deals they struck.

## 2. Split Sheets & Collaborations

- **The Golden Rule:** Never leave the studio without a signed split sheet.
- **Publishing Splits:** Equal splits (everyone in the room gets equal share) vs. Contribution splits (lyrics 50%, beat 50%).
- **Work-For-Hire:** If paying a session musician or graphic designer, use a Work-For-Hire agreement to ensure they do not own a percentage of the final master or copyright.

## 3. Clearances & Interpolations

- **Sampling:** Requires clearance from BOTH the Master owner (usually the label) and the Publishing owner (the songwriters).
- **Interpolation:** Re-recording a melody/lyric. Only requires Publishing clearance, not Master clearance.
