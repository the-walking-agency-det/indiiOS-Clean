# Creative Direction & Visual DNA (Agent Zero Skill)

**Domain Expert:** Creative Director / A&R Visuals  
**Goal:** Build a cohesive "World," design the visual narrative, and ensure aesthetic consistency across all touchpoints (Spotify canvas, artwork, press photos, socials).

## 1. The Visual DNA Grid

- **Color Palette:** 2-3 primary colors that define the "Era". (e.g., The Weeknd's "After Hours" red/black, or Billie Eilish's "Happier Than Ever" blonde/beige).
- **Typography:** One display font (for logos/headers) and one utility font (for tracklists/credits). Never use more than 2 fonts on an asset package.
- **Textural Elements:** Film grain, VHS glitches, halftones, clean vector gradients, distressed overlays.
- **The Silhouette:** Highly recognizable artists have a distinct silhouette. Think deadmau5's head, Daft Punk's helmets, or Slash's top hat. Define the primary stylistic accessory or hairstyle.

## 2. Cover Art & Packaging

- **The DSP Thumbnail Test:** Artwork must look compelling when shrunk down to 50x50 pixels on a phone screen. High contrast, distinct shapes.
- **The "Title" Debate:** Modern cover art rarely features text. Let the image speak. If text is necessary, it must be integrated into the design, not clearly layered over it like a watermark.
- **Canvases:** Vertical 8-second looping videos for Spotify/Apple. Use cinemagraphs, B-roll from the music video, or abstract 3D loops. *Never* just animate the cover art.

## 3. The Music Video Sandbox

- **Storyboarding:** Always have a 3-act structure.
  - *Act 1 (0:00 - 0:45):* Establish the world and the conflict.
  - *Act 2 (0:45 - 2:00):* Escalation, performance shots mixed with narrative.
  - *Act 3 (2:00 - End):* The climax or twist, visual payoff.
- **Lighting Dynamics:**
  - *High Key:* Bright, even lighting. Pop, upbeat, commercial.
  - *Low Key:* High contrast, harsh shadows. Rap, rock, moody R&B.
  - *Neon Noir:* Saturated blues/pinks/purples. Synthwave, electronic.
